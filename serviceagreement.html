Responsibilities of the Provider

The provider agrees to:

  • Communicate with you openly, honestly and promptly.
  • Treat you with kindness, courtesy and respect.
  • Partner with you on decisions about how your services and supports are provided.
  • Review the provision of services and support with you, at least annually, and more often if required.
  • Provide services and supports that meet your needs, circumstances, preferences and goals.
  • Provide you with the opportunity to select your preferred support workers to support you on your preferred days and times.
  • Provide you with a minimum of 24 hours’ notice, where possible, if we have to change a scheduled appointment to provide your supports.
  • Provide supports that consider your safety.
  • Implement and maintain safeguarding policies and procedures to uphold your human and legal rights.
  • Develop and review your Personal Emergency Preparation Plan with you, and use it during emergencies or disasters.
  • Follow incident management procedures in line with our Incident Management Policy, and inform you of actions and outcomes.
  • Declare and manage any conflicts of interest in line with our Conflict of Interest Policy.
  • Explain how you can provide feedback and lodge complaints, both internally and externally (e.g., NDIS Commission).
  • Listen to your feedback and resolve problems quickly.
  • Never offer or give you financial advice or recommendations.
  • Review your Support Plan and this Service Agreement when your circumstances change, and give you updated copies.
  • Ensure workers are appropriately screened, trained and supervised for the supports they deliver (including High Intensity supports).
  • Protect your privacy and personal information in line with privacy laws and our Privacy Policy.
  • Issue accurate invoices and statements for agreed supports and costs.
  • Give you the required notice if we must end this Service Agreement.

Emergency & Disaster Arrangements

If an emergency or disaster (e.g., bushfire, flood, pandemic) affects services, HD HeeD will activate your Personal Emergency Preparation Plan, prioritise essential supports, and keep you informed of any changes. We will coordinate with your support network and emergency services where needed to help keep you safe and resume services as soon as possible.

Restrictive Practices

HD HeeD does not use restrictive practices unless they are part of an authorised behaviour support plan and legally permitted. We use any restrictive practice only as a last resort, for the shortest time necessary, with your involvement and consent (or that of your authorised representative), and we actively work to reduce and eliminate any restrictive practice over time.

Service Continuity (Provider changes)

If we need to change or cancel a service (e.g., worker illness), we will give you as much notice as possible, offer a suitable replacement worker or reschedule, and ensure no fees are charged to you for provider-initiated changes.

Australian Consumer Law

HD HeeD will comply with Australian Consumer Law and the Competition and Consumer Act 2010 (Cth). Our services are fit-for-purpose and match their description. We will not:

  • Advise we can provide specialised services if we are not legally able.
  • Make false claims about the efficacy of supports, services or products.
  • Mislead or deceive by providing false or insufficient information.
  • Accept payment for services we cannot deliver or that you have not agreed to.
  • Request or accept additional fees outside of this Agreement.
  • Engage in unfair or high-pressure tactics.

NDIS Code of Conduct

HD HeeD and our workers comply with the NDIS Code of Conduct. We will:

  • Respect your rights to freedom of expression, self-determination and decision-making.
  • Respect your privacy.
  • Provide supports safely and competently.
  • Act with integrity, honesty and transparency.
  • Raise and act on concerns affecting safety or quality of supports.
  • Take reasonable steps to prevent and respond to violence, exploitation, neglect, abuse and sexual misconduct.
  • Not charge NDIS participants higher prices than non-participants for the same goods/services.

Reportable Incidents

We maintain an incident management system. If a reportable incident occurs (e.g., serious injury, abuse or neglect, unauthorised restrictive practice), we will notify the NDIS Commission as required, support you throughout, and keep you informed of actions and outcomes.

Responsibilities of the Participant / Representative

The participant/representative agrees to:

  • Respect workers’ rights and provide a safe, harassment-free environment.
  • Abide by the terms of this Agreement.
  • Accept responsibility for choices, even if they involve risk.
  • Tell us if you have problems with your care and services.
  • Give us enough information to deliver and review your Support Plan.
  • Care for your own health and wellbeing as much as possible.
  • Provide information to help us meet your needs.
  • Give at least 24 hours’ notice if you won’t be home for a scheduled service.
  • Understand workers are only authorised for agreed hours/tasks in this Agreement.
  • Participate in home safety assessments.
  • Ensure pets are controlled during visits.
  • Provide a smoke-free working environment.
  • Pay the agreed amount for services provided.
  • Give notice before ending services.
  • Inform us of how you want supports delivered.
  • Treat workers with courtesy and respect.
  • Notify us immediately if your NDIS Plan changes or ends.

Advocacy & Supported Decision-Making

You may have an advocate or support person involved in any meetings, service planning, complaints or disputes. Tell us who your advocate is and how to contact them, and we will include them (with your consent).

Pricing and Payments

HD HeeD will charge within the NDIS Pricing Arrangements and Price Limits. We will:

  • Declare prices before delivering a service.
  • Make claims truthfully and only for agreed supports in your NDIS Plan.
  • Provide receipts/statements against your NDIS Plan.
  • Request payment only after services are delivered.
  • Submit claims for NDIA-managed participants within required timeframes.
  • Seek agreement before any change to charges.
  • Keep full records of supports delivered.

Provider Travel – Labour Costs

Travel time to and from your location may be charged at the support hourly rate, capped at 30 minutes each way in metro areas and 60 minutes in regional/remote areas, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Provider Travel – Non-Labour Costs

You must reimburse vehicle running expenses at $0.78/km, plus tolls, parking, or public transport costs at actual cost with receipts. Travel costs must be pre-agreed and itemised on invoices.

Payment Options

  • Self-managed: You pay invoices within an agreed time (e.g., 7 days).
  • Plan Nominee: Your nominee pays invoices within agreed time.
  • NDIA-managed: HD HeeD claims directly from NDIA.
  • Plan-managed: HD HeeD claims from your nominated Plan Manager.

Cancellations

Short-Notice Cancellations

  • Non-DSW supports (e.g., nursing/therapy): less than 2 business days notice – may be charged up to 100% of the fee if we cannot reallocate that time.
  • DSW supports: less than 7 days notice – may be charged up to 100% of the fee if we cannot reallocate that time.
  • Groups: if you cancel and we cannot find a replacement participant, the agreed fee may be charged.
  • We may waive fees at our discretion in exceptional circumstances (e.g., sudden illness/emergency).
  • Provider-initiated changes: we will give you as much notice as possible and no fees apply.

Feedback, Complaints & Disputes

  1. Talk to us first: Contact our team on 02 8630 5500 or info@hdheed.com.au. We’ll try to resolve quickly and fairly. You may have an advocate support you.
  2. Not resolved or prefer external? Contact the NDIS Commission on 1800 035 544 or via their website. You can contact them at any time.

Goods and Services Tax (GST)

For GST purposes, the parties confirm that the supports supplied under this Agreement are reasonable and necessary supports under the participant’s current NDIS Plan. The participant will tell the provider if their plan is replaced or if they cease to be a participant in the NDIS.
