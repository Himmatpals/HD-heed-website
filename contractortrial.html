HD HeeD — Policy Acknowledgements

HD HeeD — Detailed Policy Acknowledgements

These policies form part of your Contractor Agreement.

1. NDIS Code of Conduct

You must at all times:

  • Respect the rights of people with disability to freedom of expression, self-determination and decision-making.
  • Provide supports and services in a safe, competent, and person-centred manner.
  • Respect the privacy and confidentiality of information collected about participants.
  • Act with integrity, honesty and transparency in all dealings.
  • Promptly take action on any concerns about matters that may impact safety or quality of supports.
  • Prevent and respond to violence, neglect, abuse, exploitation, or sexual misconduct.
  • Not charge participants more than a non-participant for the same service.

2. Incident Reporting & Complaints

You are required to:

  • Immediately report all incidents, injuries, hazards and near misses to the Provider.
  • Identify and escalate reportable incidents (e.g. death, serious injury, abuse, unauthorised restrictive practices) to management without delay.
  • Use emergency services first if needed, then notify the Provider and complete documentation.
  • Support participants to make complaints safely and without fear of reprisal.
  • Cooperate with investigations and implement corrective actions as directed.

3. Privacy & Confidentiality

You agree to:

  • Comply with the Privacy Act 1988 and HD HeeD’s Privacy Policy.
  • Collect and use personal information only for lawful and necessary purposes.
  • Share information only with consent or where legally required.
  • Securely store records and report breaches or suspected breaches immediately.
  • Maintain confidentiality obligations even after this Agreement ends.

4. Safeguarding & Duty of Care

As a worker you must:

  • Prioritise the safety and wellbeing of participants at all times.
  • Respect the dignity of risk by enabling informed choices while minimising harm.
  • Maintain professional boundaries — no financial, sexual or exploitative relationships.
  • Report any suspicion or knowledge of abuse or neglect immediately.
  • Work within your level of training and follow behaviour support plans where relevant.

5. Workplace Health & Safety (WHS)

Your WHS duties include:

  • Taking reasonable care for your own safety and the safety of others.
  • Following safe work policies, training, and using PPE provided.
  • Reporting hazards, incidents, injuries or near misses immediately.
  • Never working under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or extreme fatigue.
  • Participating in training, audits and reviews to improve safety standards.

Final Acknowledgement

By signing here you confirm that you have read, understood and agree to comply with all five policy areas above.
