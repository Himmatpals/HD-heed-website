Policy Acknowledgements

Read each section carefully; acknowledging means you understand and will comply.

NDIS Code of Conduct

This code requires you to:

  • Respect participants’ rights, preferences, and autonomy.
  • Maintain privacy and confidentiality.
  • Provide safe, competent, and respectful supports.
  • Be honest, act lawfully and transparently.
  • Raise concerns about safety or quality and protect against abuse, neglect, exploitation, violence.
  • Not charge NDIS participants more than non-participants for the same service.

Incident Reporting & Complaints

Workers must:

  • Report all incidents, near-misses, and injuries immediately, using the incident management system.
  • Identify and escalate reportable incidents (serious injury, abuse, unauthorised restrictive practice) without delay.
  • Use emergency services first if required, then notify the provider and follow up in writing.
  • Support participants to make complaints without fear of reprisal; treat complaints confidentially and fairly.
  • Participate in investigations and corrective actions as necessary.

Privacy & Confidentiality Policy

Key points:

  • Comply with the Privacy Act 1988 and HD HeeD’s Privacy Policy.
  • Collect, use and store personal information only as necessary and lawfully.
  • Share participant information only with consent or where required by law.
  • Keep records secure and report breaches promptly.
  • Confidentiality continues after employment/engagement ends.

Safeguarding & Duty of Care

As a worker, you will:

  • Prioritize participant safety and take all reasonable steps to avoid harm.
  • Maintain professional boundaries and never exploit your role.
  • Respect participants’ dignity of risk and support informed choice.
  • Report, without delay, any suspicion or knowledge of abuse or neglect.
  • Act only within your training and follow behaviour support plans where relevant.

Workplace Health & Safety (WHS)

This includes your duties to:

  • Take reasonable care for your own safety and that of others.
  • Follow safe work policies, training and use PPE correctly.
  • Report hazards, incidents, injuries or near misses immediately.
  • Be fit for work—no alcohol, drugs or excessive fatigue.
  • Participate in WHS improvements and audits as required.
