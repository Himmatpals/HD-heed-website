1. Parties

This agreement is between the Provider and the Contractor named below.

2. Engagement & Status

  • The Contractor is engaged as an independent contractor unless otherwise agreed in writing; not an employee.
  • The Contractor is responsible for tax, superannuation, and insurance unless expressly covered by the Provider.
  • Scope: provide NDIS supports to participants according to plans and Provider directions.
  • Maintain professional boundaries and cultural safety; keep accurate timesheets and shift records.
  • Promptly report incidents and hazards per Provider procedures.

3. Standards of Conduct

  • Comply with the NDIS Code of Conduct and all Provider policies and procedures.
  • Provide services safely, competently, respectfully and within scope of training/competency.
  • Respect participants’ rights, choices, privacy, culture and dignity of risk.

4. Screening, Training & Credentials

The Contractor must hold and maintain the following at engagement (or as applicable by law/policy), and complete induction items after starting:

  • NDIS Worker Screening Clearance; Working With Children Check (if required); National Police Check.
  • Complete Provider induction and mandatory training modules (see Induction section below). First Aid & CPR may be completed after starting and within the timeframe set in the Induction section.

5. Insurance

  • Contractor must hold (or be expressly covered under Provider’s) Public Liability and Professional Indemnity insurance in adequate amounts.
  • Workers Compensation insurance is required where applicable by law.
  • Provide evidence of cover on request and on renewal.

6. Payment & Expenses

  • Rates/fees must be agreed in writing and align with applicable NDIS pricing (where relevant).
  • Invoices must include ABN, GST status, dates/hours of service, participant initials and purchase orders (if used).
  • Provider pays within agreed cycles; incorrect invoices may be returned for correction.
  • Travel/expenses reimbursed only if pre-approved and supported by receipts.

7. Privacy & Confidentiality

  • Comply with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Provider’s Privacy Policy.
  • Collect/use/store/disclose personal information only lawfully and as necessary; share only with consent or legal basis.
  • Keep records secure; report suspected or actual breaches immediately.
  • Obligations continue after this Agreement ends; return all records/property on exit.

8. Workplace Health & Safety (WHS)

  • Take reasonable care for own and others’ safety; be fit for work; follow safe work practices and training.
  • Use PPE and infection prevention measures; report hazards, incidents, injuries and near-misses promptly.
  • Participate in WHS audits, supervision and continuous improvement activities.

9. Termination & Non-Solicitation

  • Either party may terminate with 2 weeks’ notice in writing.
  • Immediate termination may occur for serious misconduct, non-compliance, or risk to participant safety.
  • During engagement and for months after ending, the Contractor will not solicit Provider’s participants for private work without written consent.

10. Dispute Resolution & External Complaints

  • Raise concerns in writing with the Provider for timely resolution.
  • If unresolved, matters may be referred to external mediation or relevant bodies (including the NDIS Commission for NDIS-related issues).

Policy Acknowledgements (Read & Sign Each)

These policies form part of your obligations. Read each detailed summary before signing.

A. NDIS Code of Conduct

  • Respect rights and decision-making; support self-determination and dignity of risk.
  • Provide safe, competent, person-centred supports within scope.
  • Respect privacy and confidentiality; act with integrity, honesty and transparency.
  • Raise and act on concerns; prevent and respond to violence, abuse, neglect, exploitation and sexual misconduct.
  • Do not charge NDIS participants more than non-participants for the same service.

B. Incident Reporting & Complaints

  • Immediately report incidents, near-misses, injuries and hazards; complete documentation.
  • Identify and escalate reportable incidents (serious injury, abuse, unauthorised restrictive practices) without delay.
  • Call emergency services first when needed; then notify Provider and cooperate with investigations.
  • Support participant complaints without reprisal; treat complaints fairly and confidentially.
  • Implement corrective actions and procedure updates as directed.

C. Privacy & Confidentiality

  • Comply with the Privacy Act 1988 and the Provider’s Privacy Policy.
  • Collect/use/store/disclose personal information only lawfully and as necessary; share only with consent or legal basis.
  • Keep records secure; report suspected or actual breaches immediately.
  • Confidentiality continues after engagement ends; return all records/property on exit.

D. Safeguarding & Duty of Care

  • Prioritise participant safety and wellbeing; maintain professional boundaries.
  • Respect dignity of risk and enable informed choice while minimising harm.
  • Report any suspicion or knowledge of abuse/neglect immediately via incident procedures and to authorities where required.
  • Work only within training/competency and follow behaviour support plans where relevant.

E. Workplace Health & Safety (WHS)

  • Take reasonable care of your own and others’ safety; be fit for work.
  • Follow WHS procedures and training; use PPE and safe manual handling practices.
  • Report hazards, incidents, injuries and near-misses immediately.
  • Participate in WHS training, audits and improvements.
