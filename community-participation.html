Community Participation

Supporting your journey to connect and belong.

Involving You in Community Life

This service is all about helping you get out and engage with your community in ways that are meaningful and fun for you. Community participation support provides a worker to accompany or assist you in social, recreational, educational, or civic activities. The beauty of this support is that it’s very individualized – it can involve all sorts of activities depending on your interests and goals. For example, you might have a support worker help you attend a local sports club or exercise class, go to a hobby group or art class, visit the library, or just meet up with friends at a café.

Community participation can be one-on-one or in group programs where you join other people in activities. The format will depend on what you’re comfortable with and what’s available in your area.

Support workers help with:

  • Transport planning and training
  • Assistance during activities
  • Safety and accessibility arrangements
  • Encouragement and companionship

Why It Matters

  • Builds confidence and independence
  • Reduces isolation and improves mental wellbeing
  • Encourages new friendships and social skills
  • Gives carers respite while you enjoy outings

Examples of Activities

  • Joining clubs or classes like art, book clubs, or sports
  • Attending movies, concerts, or local festivals
  • Taking workshops or short courses
  • Volunteering or community service
  • Going for coffee, shopping, or park visits
