1. Standard Participant Intake (Self or Plan Managed)

Context: This is a first-contact call with an NDIS participant to welcome them and gather initial details. The participant may be self-managed or plan-managed. The tone is friendly and informative, ensuring the participant feels comfortable and informed about the process.

Key Information to Gather

  • Participant's personal details (full name, preferred name, contact information)
  • NDIS number and current plan start/end dates
  • Plan management type (self-managed vs plan-managed)
  • Supports the participant is seeking and their goals
  • Any immediate needs or preferences
  • Whether they have a support coordinator or other key person
  • Any safety or health considerations

Common Concerns & Responses

Concern: "Will the NDIS cover all these services? I'm worried about cost."

Response: "Your NDIS plan will fund the supports we provide, so there shouldn't be any out-of-pocket cost for you for the approved services. We'll make sure to stick within your plan's budget."

Concern: "I'm new to this and not sure what information you need or what happens next."

Response: "No problem at all. I'll walk you through everything step by step. In this call, I'll ask a few questions just to understand your needs and get basic details."

Suggested Next Steps

  • Provide a brief overview of the services your organization offers that are relevant to the participant's needs
  • Explain that a service agreement will be prepared for them to review and sign
  • Arrange an initial face-to-face meeting or assessment
  • Send a follow-up email or text summarizing key points of this call
  • Encourage them to reach out with any questions
