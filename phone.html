1) Standard Participant Intake Self / Plan-Managed

Warm, plain-English first call to welcome a new NDIS participant and set up supports smoothly.
Purpose Quick Checklist Typical Concerns & Responses Suggested Next Steps Full Conversation Script FAQs

Caller Type & Role: Intake Officer (admin or clinical).

Context: First contact with a participant who is self-managed or plan-managed. Goal is to make them feel welcome, collect essentials, and set clear next steps.

Quick Intake Checklist

  • Participant details: full name, preferred name, best contact.
  • NDIS number, plan start/end dates.
  • Plan management type; if plan-managed, capture plan manager contact.
  • Supports sought + goals (e.g., personal care, therapy, community access).
  • Immediate preferences (times, worker gender, communication or access needs).
  • Support Coordinator/other key contacts.
  • Safety/health considerations relevant to service delivery.

Typical Concerns & High-Trust Responses

“Will NDIS cover this? I’m worried about cost.”

Your NDIS plan funds approved supports. We’ll keep within your budget and invoice NDIA/your plan manager directly—no out-of-pocket costs for approved services.

“I’m new and not sure what happens next.”

No worries—I’ll guide you step-by-step in plain English. I’ll ask a few questions, explain what we do, and confirm next steps. Please stop me anytime.

“I have a plan manager—do I handle payments?”

No—your plan manager handles invoices and claims. We coordinate with them so you can focus on your supports, not the paperwork.

“How soon can we start?”

Right after this call we’ll set up your first meeting/assessment—often within 1–2 weeks depending on your availability. If anything is urgent, tell me and we’ll prioritise.

Suggested Next Steps
  • Give a brief overview of relevant services; send a friendly summary.
  • Prepare and send a clear, readable Service Agreement; invite questions.
  • Book the initial assessment/meet-and-greet (clinician or support worker).
  • Share your direct contact details; encourage them to reach out anytime.

Full Conversation Script (Verbatim-Ready)

Intake: “Hello, this is [Name] calling from [Your Organization]. May I please speak with [Participant Name]?”

Participant: “Speaking. Hi.”

Intake: “Hi [Participant Name]! How are you today? I’m calling because we received your details as someone interested in our NDIS support services. I wanted to introduce myself and see how we can assist you. Is now a good time to chat for about 10–15 minutes?”

Participant: “Yes, that’s fine.”

Intake: “Wonderful. First, are you self-managing your plan or do you have a plan manager?”

Participant: “I have a plan manager who handles the payments.”

Intake: “Thanks—that means we’ll invoice your plan manager directly so you won’t need to manage payments. Could I grab their name and email to coordinate billing?”

Participant: “Bright Plan Management, plan@brightpm.com.”

Intake: “Perfect, noted. Next, what supports are you hoping to set up and what would you like to achieve? For example: help at home with daily tasks, community access, or therapy like physio or OT.”

Participant: “I have a spinal injury; I need morning personal care, some physio to build strength, and help getting to the shops and appointments since I can’t drive.”

Intake: “Got it—personal care, community access, and physiotherapy. We can help with all of those. Roughly how many days a week were you thinking of support worker help?”

Participant: “Daily in the mornings for personal care, and once a week for outings.”

Intake: “Great—we’ll shape a roster that fits. We’ll also involve our physio—likely an assessment first, then regular sessions. May I confirm your NDIS number and date of birth?”

Participant: “NDIS 123456789; DOB 5 May 1980.”

Intake: “Thanks. And your plan end date—does it run until next June?”

Participant: “Yes, June next year.”

Intake: “Perfect. Do you have a Support Coordinator or anyone else you’d like us to include in communications?”

Participant: “[SC Name/contact if any].”

Intake: “Excellent. From here, we’ll book an initial meeting—either at your home or our clinic. We’ll also send a simple Service Agreement so you can read it in your own time and ask questions—no rush.”

Participant: “Okay—that sounds good. When can we meet?”

Intake: “Do afternoons suit you better? How about this Thursday at 2:00 PM? I can come with our physio, [Physio Name], to your home.”

Participant: “Yes, that works.”

Intake: “Great—locked in for Thursday 2 PM. We’ll send a reminder the day before. We take a person-centred approach: you’re in control, and we’ll adjust as we go. If you think of any questions after we hang up, just reply to my email or give me a call.”

Participant: “Thank you, I appreciate that.”

Intake: “Pleasure! To recap: Thursday 2 PM at your place; we’ll email the Service Agreement and a short summary; we’ll coordinate with your plan manager. Looking forward to working with you.”

Participant: “Thanks—talk soon. Bye.”

Intake: “Bye!”

Frequently Asked Questions
  • Do I pay anything out of pocket? No—approved supports are billed to your NDIS plan (or plan manager). We also track your budget for you.
  • I’m new to NDIS—will you guide me? Absolutely. We keep it simple, explain each step, and invite questions anytime.
  • How fast can we start? We usually schedule your first meeting within 1–2 weeks (or sooner for urgent needs).
  • What happens after this call? You’ll receive a friendly summary, our contact details, and the Service Agreement to review; then your first appointment.
