1) Standard Participant Intake Self/Plan-Managed

First-contact call to welcome a new participant, confirm plan details, and map supports. Tone: friendly, clear, empowering.

Key information to gather
  • Participant details, NDIS number, plan dates, plan management type + plan manager contacts.
  • Supports sought & goals; immediate needs & preferences (times, gender preference, communication needs).
  • Existing team (support coordinator, therapists), safety/health considerations.
Common concerns & helpful responses
Cost worries: “Your NDIS plan funds approved supports; we coordinate billing with your plan manager so you can focus on your support.”
New to NDIS: “We’ll guide you step by step, in plain language. Ask anything at any time.”
Start timing: “After intake we book your first meeting/assessment within days; we’ll prioritise urgent needs.”
Suggested next steps
  • Send service overview + draft service agreement; book initial visit/assessment.
  • Confirm roster options; share written summary with contacts & next appointment.
Conversation example (script)

Intake: “Hello, this is [Name] from [Org]. May I speak with [Participant]?”

Participant: “Speaking.”

Intake: “Great to meet you. We received your details about NDIS supports. Is now a good time for ~15 minutes?” …

(Full standard-intake dialogue, including plan manager coordination, booking first visit, and recap.)

2) Family Member / Next of Kin Calling on Behalf

When a family member is the main contact. Keep participant centred, privacy respected, and consent clear.

Key information to gather
  • Relationship & legal role (guardian/nominee) and participant’s consent to involve them.
  • Participant background, current supports, communication preferences/challenges.
  • Who remains primary contact; documents to collect (assessments, reports).
Conversation example (script)

Intake: “Hello, I’m [Name] from [Org]. Am I speaking with [Daughter/Son/etc.] regarding [Participant]’s NDIS supports?” …

(Warm acknowledgement, involve participant on speaker, outline supports, schedule home visit, draft agreement.)

3) Support Coordinator Referral

Three‑way partnership: participant (choice & control), SC (coordination), provider (delivery).

Key information to gather
  • What the SC referred for; participant’s goals in their own words; timelines/urgency.
  • How to communicate with SC (cc updates, joint meetings, reports for reviews).
Conversation example (script)

Intake: “Hi [Participant], [SC Name] asked me to connect. Is now okay?” …

(Confirm goals like showering/cooking post‑stroke; book OT weekly; schedule support worker; service agreement & bookings; pre‑review reporting.)

4) Complex Needs (Behaviours of Concern / Restrictive Practices)

Clinical intake emphasising Positive Behaviour Support, safety, dignity, and consistency.

Key information to gather
  • Behaviours, frequency, triggers; effective/ineffective strategies; PBS plan details.
  • Any restrictive practices & authorisations; safety requirements; allied team; communication style; environment.
  • Family priorities; crisis plan; data tracking approach.
Conversation example (script)

(Full specialist-led dialogue: non‑judgemental history, PRN protocol, collaboration with practitioner, consistent roster, shadowing, meet‑and‑greet with preferred interests.)

5) High‑Risk Cases (Hospital Discharge / Urgent Safety)

Mobilise support rapidly; coordinate with hospital teams; provide a 72‑hour stabilisation plan.

Key information to gather
  • Discharge date/time; condition; aftercare; equipment; discharge plan.
  • Immediate 24–72 hr supports; current supports; funding flexibility & contacts; home safety; contingency.
Conversation example (script)

(Discharge‑day plan: worker meets patient, equipment fast‑track, meals, nurse visits, pendant checks, 3‑visits‑daily for first days, friend as backup; funding reassurance; pet care.)

6) Nursing‑Only Supports (Wound Care, PEG, Catheter)

Clinical tasks delivered by qualified nurses; precision, continuity, and clear contingencies.

Key information to gather
  • Task specifics, frequency/timing; medical background; existing protocols/orders.
  • Supplies & sourcing; home setup; comfort/pain; carer training interest; contingencies; funding line items.
Conversation example (PEG focus)

(Three daily pump feeds, flushes, medication via PEG, site care, gradual skill‑sharing, continuity of nurse, on‑call, first‑visit checks, documentation.)

7) CALD Backgrounds / Interpreter Required

Language access, cultural safety, and respectful flexibility. Use interpreters at no cost to participants.

Key information to gather
  • Preferred language & interpreter needs; primary contact (family vs participant).
  • Cultural/religious practices; holidays/observances; gender preferences; dietary rules.
  • Understanding of NDIS concepts; translated materials; community connections.
Conversation example (with daughter interpreting)

(Offer TIS interpreter, confirm address terms of respect, simple English + greeting in language, schedule female worker, no‑pork/ shoes‑off, church access, translated agreement.)

9) Transitioning from Another Provider

Seamless switch with zero downtime; respectful handling; align start/end dates and bookings.

Key information to gather
  • Reason for transition; services to continue/change; end date & notice period; pending items & reports.
  • What worked vs didn’t; avoid double‑billing; new agreement & booking; first‑month review.
Conversation example (script)

(Reliability & consistency focus; two regular workers; intro visit during last shift; ‘coffee‑first‑then‑shower’ preference; dedicated cleaning visit; plan‑manager coordination.)

11) Aged Care Crossover (My Aged Care + NDIS)

Use both systems complementarily; coordinate with aged‑care case manager; avoid duplication.

Key information to gather
  • Age & status (continuing participant post‑65); list of aged‑care vs NDIS services; overlaps/gaps; contacts.
  • Clarify coverage (equipment/therapy); joint meeting & shared plan; educate that NDIS continues post‑65.
Conversation example (script)

(Map HCP nursing/cleaning vs NDIS personal care/physio/equipment; add one NDIS hour for chores; share physio notes with nurse; joint call; adjustable bed pathway.)

Frequently Asked Questions

How fast can services start?
Often within days after intake; high‑risk cases can be same‑day where feasible.
Do you use interpreters?
Yes — professional interpreters are available at no cost to participants when needed.
Will I get the same worker?
We prioritise continuity; you’ll meet your core team and approve any backup in advance.
What if funding changes mid‑plan?
We’ll re‑budget with you, update agreements, and adjust rosters promptly.
Can you coordinate with hospital/school/aged‑care?
Absolutely, with your consent we collaborate so supports feel seamless.
What if a worker is late or can’t attend?
We notify immediately, arrange substitutes, and provide clear on‑call contacts.
Can family be involved?
Yes — as much or as little as you want, always centring the participant’s choice.
Do you help at plan reviews?
We provide progress notes/reports and suggestions based on outcomes.
