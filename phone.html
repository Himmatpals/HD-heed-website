0) Pre‑call checklist

  • Open the Referral form: hdheed.com.au/referral
  • Have the NDIS price limits and service list handy.
  • Prepare the Consent & Privacy statement (below) to read verbatim.
  • Keep calendar open for assessment/meet‑and‑greet booking.
  • Check service area (Blacktown → Greater Sydney).
Goal of the call: Confirm eligibility & fit, collect key info, secure referral submission or book an assessment.

Step‑by‑Step Call Flow

  1. Warm greeting & orientation — who you are, RN‑led provider, quick outline of the call (2–3 mins).
  2. Identify caller role — Participant, family/NOK, or Support Coordinator (SC)/referrer.
  3. Consent & Privacy — read the consent script before collecting personal/health info.
  4. High‑level need — what supports are needed and why now?
  5. Eligibility & funding — NDIS status, plan dates, management (self/plan/NDIA), budgets available.
  6. Safeguarding snapshot — risks, behaviours of concern, clinical alerts, equipment, environment.
  7. Preferences & logistics — days/times, location(s), language/interpreter, gender preference, start date.
  8. Fit check & pricing — confirm we can support; outline pricing at NDIS limits; transparency on travel/cancellations.
  9. Close with action — send the referral link or complete it on the call; book assessment/meet‑and‑greet.
  10. Wrap‑up — recap next steps; provide contact details and response time.
  11. After‑call admin — document notes, create tasks, send summary email/SMS.

Core Phone Script (read‑ready)

1) Greeting & orientation

Hi, you’re speaking with [Your name] from HD HeeD, an RN‑led NDIS provider based in Blacktown supporting the Greater Sydney area. Is now a good time to talk for about five minutes? I’ll ask a few questions to understand your needs and funding setup, then we can book a next step or I can send you our quick referral link.

2) Caller role

Just so I tailor this properly — are you the NDIS participant, a family member or next of kin, or a Support Coordinator/referrer?

3) Consent & privacy (read verbatim)

Before we continue, I’ll read a quick consent message. We’ll collect only the information needed to discuss services, check eligibility, and arrange next steps. We store information securely and handle it according to the NDIS Code of Conduct and privacy laws. You can ask us to correct or delete your information at any time. Do you consent to proceed?

4) High‑level need & goals

Could you share what support you’re looking for and what a good outcome would look like? For example: personal care, community access, skill‑building, nursing support, or something else?

5) Eligibility & funding

Are you currently an NDIS participant? If yes: what are your plan dates, and is your plan self‑managed, plan‑managed (by which provider?), or NDIA‑managed? Do you know which budgets you want to use for these supports?

6) Safeguarding snapshot

To make sure we keep everyone safe, are there any risks or behaviours of concern we should know about? Any medical alerts like allergies, diabetes, epilepsy; equipment such as hoist, wheelchair, or oxygen; or environmental considerations at home?

7) Preferences & logistics

Which suburb will services be delivered in? What days and times suit you best? Do you have a preference for a male or female worker, language or interpreter needs, or specific cultural preferences?

8) Fit check & pricing

Thanks — based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like we can help. We charge at the current NDIS price limits for the relevant support item, and we’re transparent about travel and cancellation rules. Would you like us to proceed with a referral and book a meet‑and‑greet or assessment?

9) Close with action

I can text or email you our quick referral link now — hdheed.com.au/referral — or I can complete it with you on this call. What do you prefer?

10) Wrap‑up

Great — I’ll summarise today’s notes and next steps. If you need anything in the meantime, call 02 8630 5500 or email info@hdheed.com.au. Thanks for your time today.

Caller Variants

Participant variant

Softening questions: What matters most to you in a support worker? Have you had supports before — what worked, what didn’t?

Enablement: Would you like us to show you how to view budgets or read item numbers so you always feel in control?

Family / NOK variant

Capacity & consent: Does the participant consent to you speaking on their behalf today? If not, do you have formal authority (e.g., guardian)?

Relief & safety: What are the immediate concerns you want us to stabilise first?

Support Coordinator (SC) / Referrer variant

Scope & urgency: Which registration groups and ratios are required? Any behaviour / nursing flags? Target start date?

Docs requested: Plan summary pages, risk/behaviour plans, OT/physio reports, consent to share, SC contact details for coordination.

Objection Handling (quick replies)

“We already have a provider.”
That’s great — our aim is to fit where needed. If there are gaps (e.g., after‑hours, nursing, or short‑notice), we can complement your current provider. Would it help if I emailed a simple overview and our referral link for when a gap pops up?
“What do you charge?”
We bill at the current NDIS price limits for the relevant support item. We’re transparent about travel and cancellations up‑front so there are no surprises. If you like, I can include the exact item numbers we’d use for your supports.
“We need someone urgently.”
Understood. Let’s confirm safety and minimum details now; I’ll escalate to scheduling right after this call and aim to offer earliest slots. If today isn’t possible, I’ll give you the first available option and keep you updated.
“Can you handle complex nursing tasks?”
Yes — we’re RN‑led and confident with catheter care, stoma care, wound care, diabetes support, and medication administration. We’ll review clinical documents and complete a nursing assessment before commencing.

After‑call Admin (internal)

  • Write progress note / CRM entry with date, caller, key info, and agreed next steps.
  • Send SMS or email with referral link and summary.
  • Set task to follow up in 24–48 hours if referral not received.
  • If urgent, notify scheduling and clinical lead immediately.
  • When referral arrives: start onboarding checklist and service agreement draft.

Do / Don’t

Do

  • Use plain language; check understanding frequently.
  • Read the consent statement verbatim before collecting health info.
  • Confirm management type and budgets early.
  • Document risks and clinical alerts clearly.
  • Close the call with a concrete next action.

Don’t

  • Promise start dates before scheduling confirms capacity.
  • Collect unnecessary health information you don’t need yet.
  • Skip recap; always restate who will do what by when.
  • Forget to send the referral link or book the assessment.
