1) Standard Participant Intake (Self or Plan Managed) Caller: Intake Officer

Context: First-contact call to welcome a new NDIS participant (self-managed or plan-managed), gather essentials, and outline next steps in warm, plain English.

Key Information to Gather

  • Participant details: full name, preferred name, DOB, best contact.
  • NDIS number; plan start/end dates; management type (self / plan / agency).
  • Plan manager details if plan-managed (name, email/phone) for billing.
  • Supports sought & goals (e.g., daily living help, therapy, community access).
  • Immediate needs & preferences (times, worker gender, comms needs).
  • Involved people: support coordinator, family/key contacts.
  • Safety/health considerations (mobility, allergies, behaviours, access).

Common Concerns & Helpful Responses

“Will the NDIS cover all these services?”
Your NDIS plan funds the approved supports we deliver, so there shouldn’t be out-of-pocket costs for those. We’ll keep services within your budget and invoice NDIS/your plan manager directly so you can focus on the support, not the paperwork.
“I’m new and not sure what happens next.”
No problem — I’ll guide you step by step. Today I’ll ask a few questions to understand your needs, then outline relevant services and next steps in plain English.
“I have a plan manager. Do I handle payments?”
Since you’re plan‑managed, we’ll invoice your plan manager directly and coordinate with them. You won’t need to manage the financial paperwork.
“How soon can we start?”
After intake we’ll book an initial assessment/meet‑and‑greet, often within 1–2 weeks depending on availability. Tell me if anything is urgent and we’ll prioritise.

Suggested Next Steps

  • Explain relevant services (e.g., OT, physio, support workers) and prepare a clear service agreement summary.
  • Schedule first assessment/meet‑and‑greet and confirm preferences.
  • Send a simple recap by email/SMS with contacts and appointment details.
  • Invite questions any time; keep communication open and friendly.

Scripted Dialogue (Warm & Plain English)

Intake: Hello, this is [Name] from [Organisation]. May I speak with [Participant Name]?

Participant: Speaking, hi.

Intake: Hi [Participant Name]! We received your details for NDIS supports and I wanted to introduce myself and see how we can help. Is now a good time for about 10–15 minutes?

Participant: Yes, that’s fine.

Intake: Great. Are you self‑managed or plan‑managed?

Participant: Plan‑managed.

Intake: Thanks — that means we’ll send invoices to your plan manager directly. Could I grab their name and email?

Participant: Bright Plan Management — plan@brightpm.com.

Intake: Noted. What supports are you hoping for — daily living help, therapy, getting out and about?

Participant: Morning personal care most days, weekly outings, and some physio.

Intake: Perfect — we can help with all of those. Roughly how many mornings per week?

Participant: Daily for mornings; outings once a week.

Intake: Great. Could I note your NDIS number and DOB?

Participant: 123456789; 5 May 1980.

Intake: Thanks. I see your plan runs to next June — we’ll align to that. Next step: we’ll book an initial meeting — afternoons okay?

Participant: Afternoons are best.

Intake: How about Thursday 2:00 pm? I can attend with our physio [Name].

Participant: That works.

Intake: Excellent. I’ll send a recap and our service agreement to review at your pace. Any questions for me right now?

Participant: No, that covers it.

Intake: Wonderful — we’re looking forward to working with you!

2) Family Member or Next of Kin Calling on Behalf Caller: Intake Officer

Context: A family member/guardian is coordinating. Balance warmth, consent, and participant choice & control.

Key Information to Gather

  • Relationship/role (guardian, nominee, informal carer) and consent status.
  • Participant details (name, age, NDIS number) and communication preferences.
  • Supports sought; current situation and goals from the family perspective.
  • Any risks/safety considerations; preferred contact plan; who to include.

Common Concerns & Helpful Responses

“Can I handle discussions on their behalf?”
Absolutely — with [Participant]’s okay (or your legal authority), we’ll work with you as primary contact while keeping [Participant] centred.
“Do I need official documents?”
For intake, informal consent is fine; we’ll sort any nominee/guardian paperwork during onboarding.
Privacy & respect
We only share details with people the participant consents to. We involve family as partners while upholding the participant’s rights and preferences.

Suggested Next Steps

  • Say hello to the participant if possible (build connection).
  • Plan an in‑person meeting including participant and family.
  • Arrange consent/nominee forms later if needed.
  • Gather relevant documents (assessments, plans).
  • Agree communication flow (who’s CC’d; how to contact).

Scripted Dialogue

Intake: Hello, this is [Name] from [Organisation]. May I speak with [Family Member Name]? I understand you’re the best contact regarding [Participant Name]’s NDIS supports.

Family: Yes, I’m [Participant]’s daughter. Thanks for calling.

Intake: Thanks for helping coordinate. (Warmly) Hi [Participant Name]! I’m [Name] from [Org] — we’ll chat together to arrange supports you’re comfortable with.

Family: He’s on speaker — he has some hearing difficulty, so I handle calls.

Intake: Understood. What prompted you to reach out and what supports are you hoping for?

Family: He had a stroke; we’d like regular in‑home support for meals and company. He already has physio/OT elsewhere.

Intake: We can help with daily living and community access. Any interests we can match on?

Family: He loves gardening and cricket.

Intake: Great — we’ll look to match a worker who enjoys those. Is his plan self/plan/agency‑managed?

Family: Agency‑managed.

Intake: Perfect — we’ll create service bookings and claim directly. Rough hours in mind?

Family: About 3 hrs/day Mon–Fri around midday; ~$20k core available.

Intake: That’s workable. We’ll monitor budget and adjust if needed. Are you happy to be primary contact for scheduling and updates?

Family: Yes — email for records, phone/text for urgent matters.

Intake: Noted. Next: we’ll organise a home visit and bring a potential worker to introduce. We’ll send a simple service agreement for review beforehand.

Family: Wednesday 3 pm works.

Intake: Locked in. If you can have any OT transfer notes handy (e.g., safe transfers), that helps our team support him safely.

Family: We have an instruction sheet — we’ll have it ready.

3) Support Coordinator Referral Caller: Intake Officer

Context: Participant referred by their Support Coordinator (SC). Clarify goals, roles, and three‑way communication.

Key Information to Gather

  • Participant’s understanding of referral and desired services.
  • SC name/organisation; permission to include them in comms.
  • Goals in participant’s own words; timelines/urgencies flagged by SC.
  • Plan management; other providers involved; preferred scheduling.

Common Concerns & Helpful Responses

Coordination burden
We’ll coordinate directly with your SC (with your permission) so you never have to relay messages — we’ll keep everyone aligned.
Who to contact for what
Tell either us or your SC — we’ll loop each other in as needed so you don’t repeat yourself.

Suggested Next Steps

  • Review referral info; acknowledge contact to SC after the call.
  • Offer joint meeting if helpful; confirm reporting cadence (e.g., before plan review).
  • Draft service agreement; set service bookings; confirm schedule.

Scripted Dialogue

Intake: Hi [Participant], I’m [Name] from [Org]. [SC Name] asked me to call about supports.

Participant: Yes, my SC mentioned you. I’m keen on OT and some help at home.

Intake: Brilliant. What are your top goals for the next six months?

Participant: Shower independently and get back to cooking simple meals.

Intake: Our OT will assess home safety/equipment and build strategies; a support worker can practise skills with you between sessions. Happy for us to keep your SC in the loop?

Participant: Yes, please CC her.

Intake: Great. OT on Wednesdays at 10 am okay? We’ll introduce a support worker for two afternoons a week to start.

Participant: Works for me.

Intake: I’ll send a service agreement and email summary today and confirm with your SC. We’ll review progress a month before your plan review.

4) Complex Needs (Behaviours of Concern / Restrictive Practices) Caller: Senior Intake / Clinical

Context: Sensitive, safety‑focused intake for high‑support needs. Use Positive Behaviour Support (PBS), dignity, and non‑judgement.

Key Information to Gather

  • Behaviours: what they look like, frequency, triggers/early signs.
  • Effective/ineffective strategies; calming techniques; sensory needs.
  • Positive Behaviour Support Plan (PBSP) details; practitioner contact.
  • Restrictive practices used (if any), authorisation status, protocols.
  • Staff safety requirements; training; environmental considerations.
  • Current clinicians (psych, psych‑iatry, behaviour support) and coordination.
  • Goals beyond safety (skills, participation, quality of life).

Common Concerns & Helpful Responses

“Providers won’t stick around.”
Our team is trained for complex needs and views behaviour as communication. We build trust, apply PBS, and commit to consistency.
“Can staff handle meltdowns?”
We brief workers on triggers, early signs, and de‑escalation; follow plans exactly; and use any restrictive practice only if authorised and absolutely necessary, aiming to reduce over time.
Medication (PRN) worries
We follow the prescribed protocol as a last resort, prioritising least‑restrictive strategies and reviewing any use to learn and prevent.

Suggested Next Steps

  • Comprehensive multi‑party meeting; obtain PBSP, incident/safety plans, med list.
  • Specialised training/briefing; assign a small consistent team; shadowing.
  • Collaborative crisis plan; data tracking for triggers/patterns.

Scripted Dialogue

Intake (Clinical): Thanks for speaking with me about [Participant]. This is a judgement‑free chat to understand what works and what doesn’t.

Family: He has autism and occasional aggressive outbursts, often with transitions.

Intake: What helps in the moment and what makes it worse?

Family: Space, removing throwables, and talking about trains helps. PRN is authorised but rare.

Intake: We’ll obtain the PBSP and align with it, using PRN only per protocol. We’ll assign trained staff, start with a meet‑and‑greet around his interests, and keep a small consistent team. Can we book Thursday 3 pm in the backyard with his trains?

Family: That’s ideal.

Intake: Great — we’ll bring a worker who’s read up on trains, plus I’ll email a document list (PBSP, med list) and draft the service agreement noting adherence to the plan.

5) High‑Risk Cases (Hospital Discharge / Urgent Safety) Caller: Nurse / Experienced Coordinator

Context: Time‑critical planning for discharge or urgent safety risks. Keep calm, specific, and action‑oriented.

Key Information to Gather

  • Nature of risk; discharge details; aftercare needs; discharge plan availability.
  • Immediate 24–72h supports required (visits, overnight, equipment).
  • Current supports and gaps; NDIS funding to flex; key contacts (hospital/SC).
  • Home safety; contingency if timings slip; emergency contacts.

Common Concerns & Helpful Responses

“I’ll be alone after discharge.”
We’ll meet you at discharge, accompany you home, schedule multiple visits day one, and provide an on‑call number. You won’t be left alone.
“House isn’t ready.”
We’ll fast‑track essential equipment (e.g., portable ramp, shower chair) and temporary solutions while permanent items are arranged.
“Who helps overnight?”
Options include evening/morning visits, brief overnight checks, or on‑call plus a personal alarm pendant; we’ll tailor to your risk level.

Suggested Next Steps

  • Confirm roster coverage; liaise with hospital; create a written 72‑hour plan.
  • Exchange all contacts; fast‑track agreement/service bookings; set a 1‑week review.

Scripted Dialogue

Nurse: I see you’re scheduled home tomorrow morning. Biggest worries?

Participant: Wheelchair access, meals, being alone.

Nurse: We’ll install a portable ramp, deliver a shower commode, meet you at 10 am, stay several hours to settle you in, and return at 5 pm for dinner support. For nights, we’ll rely on your pendant and our on‑call — we’ll test it together.

Participant: That eases my mind. Thank you.

6) Nursing‑Only Supports (Wound Care, PEG, Catheter) Caller: Intake Nurse

Context: Clinical tasks requiring qualified nurses. Emphasise competence, sterility, continuity, and teaching at client pace.

Key Information to Gather

  • Specific task(s), frequency/timing, protocols/orders, and supplies.
  • Medical background and current regime; pain/comfort; environment set‑up.
  • Family involvement/training interest; contingencies (e.g., tube issues).

Common Concerns & Helpful Responses

“Will the nurse be truly skilled in my task?”
Yes — we assign experienced nurses in that exact task and follow doctor/dietitian plans, with senior RN oversight.
“Home isn’t like hospital.”
We bring the essentials and maintain hospital‑grade hygiene and process at home.
Continuity
We prioritise a primary nurse with one trained backup to avoid constant change.

Suggested Next Steps

  • Initial assessment visit; verify orders; document care; schedule aligned to regimen.
  • Education as desired; supply ordering; on‑call for issues.

Scripted Dialogue

Intake Nurse: I understand you have a PEG with three feeds daily via pump — 8 am, 1 pm, 7 pm. We’ll handle set‑up, site care, meds via PEG, and gradually teach at your pace if you wish.

Participant: I’m nervous doing it alone.

Intake Nurse: We’ll start full‑support, then step back only when you’re comfortable. I’ll be your primary nurse; [Backup Name] is your familiar backup. Monday 7:45 am for the first visit works?

Participant: Yes — thank you.

7) CALD Backgrounds / Interpreter Required Caller: Intake Officer

Context: Remove language barriers; show cultural respect; keep explanations simple; arrange interpreters as needed.

Key Information

  • Preferred language and interpreter need (ongoing? phone/onsite?).
  • Primary contact person; cultural/religious preferences; gender preference.
  • Holidays/observances; understanding of NDIS terms; existing community supports.

Scripted Dialogue (with family interpreter)

Intake: Thank you for translating today. We can arrange a professional interpreter at no cost next time. Are there cultural preferences we should respect at home (e.g., shoes off, dietary rules, preferred worker gender)?

Daughter: Vietnamese language preferred; no pork; shoes off; female worker please.

Intake: Noted. We’ll try to match a Vietnamese‑speaking worker; otherwise we’ll use an interpreter for visits and keep communication simple and clear.

8) Disengaged or Hard‑to‑Reach Clients Caller: Outreach Intake

Context: Gentle, non‑pressuring contact; make engagement easy; leave the door open.

Voicemail Template

Voicemail: Hi [Name], I’m [Your Name] from [Org]. We received a referral to see if NDIS support might help. No pressure — just here if you’re interested. I’ll try again next week, or you can call/text me anytime. Take care.

Brief Conversation Example

Intake: I can give a 60‑second overview and you decide if we continue?

Participant: Okay, one minute.

Intake: We help with daily tasks, getting out, or whatever makes life easier. If nothing fits right now, that’s fine; I can text you my number and check in next month?

Participant: A text is fine. Check in next month.

9) Clients Transitioning from Another Provider Caller: Intake Coordinator

Context: Ensure continuity; avoid gaps/overlap; address prior pain points; communicate clearly.

Key Information

  • Reason for change; prior services/hours; end date/notice; open bookings.
  • What worked/didn’t; any staff transferring; pending items or reports.

Scripted Dialogue

Intake: We’ll start the day after your last shift with [Old Provider]. You’ll meet Alice Friday 9:30 am so Saturday’s start is smooth. Any must‑dos for your morning routine?

Participant: Coffee first, then shower — previous workers ignored that.

Intake: Noted — coffee first. We’ll also schedule a dedicated cleaning visit Wednesdays 1 pm with Brenda and confirm your new service booking with your plan manager.

10) Plan Review / Change in Supports Caller: Service Coordinator

Context: Adjust services to new budgets; reduce anxiety; optimise usage; add new supports where funded.

Key Information

  • What changed (↑/↓ by category); new/removed items; participant priorities.
  • Timeline/effective date; service agreement addendum; bookings rollover.

Scripted Dialogue

Coordinator: Core has reduced ~2 hours/week; we’ll protect weekday mornings and trim Saturdays. Capacity Building increased — shall we restart weekly physio Monday 2 pm and add the Wed 3 pm social group?

Participant: Yes — let’s try that, and revisit psych therapy next month.

Coordinator: I’ll update the agreement and bookings and CC your SC. We’ll review usage in four weeks.

11) Aged Care Crossover (NDIS + My Aged Care) Caller: Intake/Case Manager

Context: Coordinate NDIS disability supports with aged care package services; avoid duplication; reassure continuity beyond 65.

Key Information

  • Aged care services in place; NDIS services/goals; overlaps/gaps; case manager contact.
  • Use NDIS for disability‑specific needs; aged care for general supports; map who funds what.

Scripted Dialogue

Intake: We’ll add an extra hour of NDIS support for meal prep/light chores to complement your Home Care cleaning. With your OK, our physio will share exercise notes with the nurse, and I’ll set a three‑way call with your aged care coordinator.

12) Paediatric Clients (Under 7, ECEI Transition) Caller: Early Childhood Coordinator

Context: Smooth handover from ECEI to standard pathway around age 7; keep play‑based, family‑centred therapy; align with school.

Key Information

  • Current therapies/reports; school transition needs; parent priorities; new LAC contact.
  • Schedule around school; consider small social playgroup; parent training.

Scripted Dialogue

Coordinator: Weekly speech with [Therapist] (school visit monthly), weekly OT for dressing/toothbrushing skills, and a trial of Saturday social playgroup. I’ll join an intro call with your LAC and the teacher, and send a straightforward service agreement for review.
