Part 1

General Guidelines & Standard Participant Inquiry

Use these model answers during first-contact calls with participants. Keep tone warm, clear, and person-centred.

Prompt Universal Standards for NDIS Intake Calls
  • Empathy & Listening: greet, introduce, and let the caller finish before responding.
  • Clarity: avoid jargon; explain essentials (service agreement, plan mgmt) in plain English.
  • Person-centred: ask open questions to map goals, routines, and preferences.
  • Key data: caller/participant details, plan status & management, suburb, supports sought, urgency.
  • Close: confirm next steps, timeframes, and preferred contact method.

This framing underpins all Q&As below.

Q1 What services do you provide under NDIS?

Answer: Personal care, domestic assistance, community participation, and RN-led nursing for disability-related health needs. We tailor supports to your goals after a brief assessment and plan together.

Q2 Are you a registered NDIS provider?

Answer: Yes—fully NDIS Registered. If you’re Agency-managed we claim via service bookings; Plan-managed we invoice your plan manager; Self-managed we invoice you to claim back.

Q3 How much experience do you have? How do I know I can trust your service?

Answer: We’ve supported a wide range of needs for years, maintain NDIS registration through external audits, and operate with clinical oversight. We prioritise reliable rosters, fast communication, and transparent pricing.

Q4 Who are your staff? Are they qualified?

Answer: Workers are screened (Police/WWCC), trained (manual handling, meds, hoists/AT), and receive ongoing education. We match workers to your preferences (language, gender), with RN oversight for health tasks.

Q5 What areas do you service?

Answer: Greater Sydney, prioritising Blacktown/Western Sydney. Share your suburb and preferred times and we’ll confirm capacity. If outside area, we’ll recommend suitable local providers.

Q6 How soon can you start?

Answer: After today’s call we book your assessment and draft a Service Agreement. Starts are often within a few days (subject to roster fit/your schedule). If urgent (e.g., hospital discharge), we’ll prioritise.

Q7 What’s the process to get started?
  • Assessment (phone or in-person) to map goals/routines.
  • Service Agreement (plain-English terms, price-guide rates, notice period).
  • Worker matching + intros if you want.
  • Commencement + early check-ins to optimise schedule.
Q8 Do I have to sign a Service Agreement?

Answer: It’s best practice and protects your choice & control—sets supports, rates, cancellations, and how to change/exit (commonly 14-day notice). We review it together and keep it simple.

Q9 How are services billed and paid?
  • Agency-managed: we claim via the NDIS portal with a service booking.
  • Plan-managed: we invoice your plan manager.
  • Self-managed: we invoice you; you claim from NDIS.

No hidden sign-up/admin fees; we follow NDIS Pricing Arrangements.

Q10 Do you charge travel or extra fees?

Answer: Only as permitted by NDIS (e.g., travel time/distance caps). Any such items are quoted upfront in the Service Agreement—no surprises.

Q11 What is your cancellation policy?

Answer: We align with NDIS cancellation rules (7 clear days) unless our agreement is more flexible. Tell us ASAP; we try to avoid charges where possible.

Q12 Can I stop or switch providers later?

Answer: Yes—NDIS is about choice and control. Provide the notice period in the agreement (commonly 14 days). We’ll support a smooth handover if you request it.

Q13 Why should I choose your service?

Answer: RN-led, person-centred care, reliable rosters, fast communication, transparent pricing, and consistent workers—professional systems with a family-run heart.

Q14 What are the next steps if I want to proceed?

Answer: Let’s book your assessment (e.g., Thu 10:00). We’ll email a summary + draft Service Agreement today and start worker matching. You can reply to the email/SMS or call anytime.

Part 2A

Family Member or Advocate Inquiry

When a parent, guardian, or advocate calls on behalf of the participant. Balance inclusion of family with respect for the participant’s autonomy.

Q1 I’m calling for my [son/daughter/parent]. Can I arrange services for them?

Answer: Yes—absolutely. We often work with family members during intake. May I have your name and relationship to [Participant]? We’ll want [Participant] involved as much as possible in decisions, but your support is valued and welcomed.

Q2 My relative isn’t communicative / has intellectual disability. How will you ensure they’re looked after?

Answer: We specialise in supporting people with communication difficulties. We learn each person’s cues and work closely with families. Staff are trained in supported decision-making, ensuring the participant’s voice is respected in all care.

Q3 Will I be kept informed?

Answer: With [Participant]’s consent, yes. We can provide updates by phone, email, or secure notes. We confirm the preferred communication method during onboarding so families always feel included.

Q4 What if my family member refuses support on a given day?

Answer: We respect participant choice. Our staff gently encourage or adapt the plan for the day. If refusal is repeated, we review strategies with you and [Participant] to find solutions together.

Q5 How do you safeguard against abuse or neglect?

Answer: All staff hold mandatory NDIS Worker Screening and police checks. We follow strict safeguarding policies and NDIS Practice Standards. Any concern raised is reported, documented, and escalated immediately.

Q6 What happens if there is an emergency while staff are with my family member?

Answer: Staff are trained in first aid and follow clear escalation protocols. If urgent, they call 000 first, then notify family and our office. An incident report is completed within 24 hours and you are updated immediately.

Q7 Will the same staff come each time?

Answer: Yes, we prioritise continuity of care. You’ll usually see the same small team of workers. If a replacement is needed, we inform you in advance and introduce them properly.

Q8 Do you offer after-hours or weekend services?

Answer: Yes, we provide supports 24/7 including weekends and public holidays. Some rates differ under the NDIS Price Guide—we always confirm costs upfront.

Q9 How do you document what happens in each shift?

Answer: Staff complete detailed shift notes after every visit—covering care provided, any incidents, and progress toward goals. These are stored securely and can be shared with you (with consent).

Part 2B

Support Coordinator or Professional Referral

When a Support Coordinator (SC), LAC, or professional refers a participant. Calls are efficient, info-heavy, and businesslike.

Q1 We have a participant needing [Service] in [Location]. Do you have capacity?

Answer: Thank you for contacting us. We do provide [Service] in [Location]. Capacity depends on staff roster—if immediate start isn’t possible, we provide clear timeframes. We’ll prioritise urgent needs where possible.

Q2 The participant has complex needs (behaviours / medical). Can your team handle this?

Answer: Yes. Our workforce includes RNs and behaviour-trained support workers. We request behaviour support or medical plans before starting, and ensure staff have completed required competencies.

Q3 How do you handle onboarding and communication?

Answer: We complete intake, develop a Service Agreement, and commence supports. You’ll receive timely updates and incident reports as required. We maintain professional collaboration and meet NDIS compliance obligations.

Q4 Do you provide progress reports for plan reviews?

Answer: Yes. We can prepare progress reports detailing outcomes, hours used, and recommendations. These align with NDIS review requirements and help participants secure appropriate future funding.

Q5 What is your escalation process if issues arise?

Answer: We encourage open communication. Minor issues are addressed directly with the participant or family. For major concerns, we escalate to management, document the matter, and inform the SC promptly. We follow the NDIS Complaints and Incident Management framework.

Part 3A

Participants with Complex or High Needs

Scenarios involving multiple disabilities, behaviours of concern, high physical support needs, or advanced medical interventions.

Q1 My family member requires 2:1 support and hoist transfers. Do you have staff for this?

Answer: Yes. We maintain a pool of staff trained in manual handling, hoist operations, and 2:1 complex support. A manual handling plan and risk assessment are developed before services commence. We also ensure staff complete competency refreshers annually and check equipment is serviced and safe.

Q2 The participant has behaviours of concern. How do you manage this?

Answer: We work strictly under Behaviour Support Plans approved by the NDIS Commission. Staff are trained in Positive Behaviour Support, trauma-informed care, and de-escalation. We use proactive strategies to reduce triggers, track behaviour data, and collaborate with practitioners to review outcomes regularly.

Q3 They require complex health supports (PEG feeding, diabetes, seizures). Can you provide this?

Answer: Yes. Our RNs and delegated workers are competent in PEG feeding, tracheostomy care, insulin administration, seizure management, and continence care. Supports are delivered under “High Intensity Daily Personal Activities” NDIS line items. Staff complete mandatory competency assessments and follow care protocols aligned with treating clinicians’ orders.

Q4 How do you ensure safety with complex cases?

Answer: Each participant has an individualised Care and Risk Plan. This includes emergency contacts, escalation procedures, and safety alerts (e.g., allergies, seizure protocols). Plans are shared with staff through our secure platform and updated monthly, or immediately after changes in condition.

Q5 Do you coordinate with multiple professionals (GP, OT, speech therapist)?

Answer: Yes. We liaise with all allied health and medical practitioners to ensure a team approach. We attend case conferences if requested, share progress notes, and integrate therapy recommendations into daily routines. This ensures consistency and goal alignment across disciplines.

Q6 Can you provide respite for carers in complex cases?

Answer: Yes. We arrange in-home respite or short-term accommodation (STA) depending on participant goals and plan funding. Staff trained in complex care provide safe continuity so families can rest knowing care needs are fully met.

Q7 What about transport for complex participants?

Answer: We provide transport supports using modified vehicles if needed (wheelchair accessible). Our staff are trained in safe transfers and vehicle safety. Travel costs are billed under NDIS transport or provider travel line items in compliance with the Price Guide.

Part 3B

Hospital Discharge or Post-Acute Support

Supporting participants leaving hospital to return home safely, avoiding readmission, and ensuring continuity of care.

Q1 Can you start supports immediately on discharge?

Answer: We mobilise staff as quickly as possible—often within 24–48 hours. For urgent discharges, we escalate to management. If immediate capacity is unavailable, we liaise with hospital social workers for interim supports to ensure no unsafe gaps.

Q2 The participant needs wound care and medication management. Do you provide this?

Answer: Yes. Our RNs can perform wound dressings, administer medications, and monitor vitals. We follow discharge summaries and keep GPs updated. All care is documented for compliance and participant safety.

Q3 How do you prepare for a safe transition home?

Answer: We review the hospital discharge plan, confirm home safety (ramps, rails, AT), arrange equipment delivery (pressure mattresses, hoists), and ensure staff are rostered to meet the participant at home. We also complete medication reconciliation and confirm family communication preferences.

Q4 Who funds post-discharge supports?

Answer: Acute medical services remain funded by the health system. Disability-related daily supports (personal care, community access, wound maintenance) are NDIS-funded. We clarify line items at intake to avoid billing confusion and document clearly in the Service Agreement.

Q5 Can you manage palliative discharges?

Answer: Yes. We support participants with palliative care needs in line with hospital and GP directions. RNs oversee comfort care, medication, and family support. Staff are trained in end-of-life care with an emphasis on dignity, comfort, and family involvement.

Q6 Do you provide transport from hospital to home?

Answer: Yes. We can arrange accessible transport, including stretcher or wheelchair transport where required. This is billed under appropriate NDIS or health funding streams, depending on the context.

Q7 How do you ensure staff are ready for post-discharge care?

Answer: Before discharge, staff are briefed on the participant’s medical and functional needs. Competencies are checked (e.g., wound care, medication handling). Where required, shadow shifts or RN supervision occur before independent support begins.

Q8 How do you prevent readmission?

Answer: By monitoring early warning signs, completing daily shift reports, maintaining medication adherence, and escalating concerns promptly to GPs or hospitals. We also educate families and participants on what to watch for at home.

Part 4A

Nursing-Only Services Inquiry

For participants whose primary or sole NDIS-funded need is nursing care (catheter, stoma, wound care, medication).

Q1 My participant only needs nursing care, not general support workers. Do you provide this?

Answer: Yes. We offer Registered Nurse supports under NDIS for disability-related health care. Examples include catheter changes, continence care, wound management, PEG/tracheostomy care, and diabetes management. We can tailor purely nursing-focused rosters if that’s all required.

Q2 Will the same nurse come each time?

Answer: We aim for continuity—usually one or two nurses assigned. If another RN is required, we provide proper handover and introduce them to ensure seamless care.

Q3 Is nursing funded by NDIS or Health?

Answer: If the health need is directly related to the participant’s disability, NDIS covers it (e.g., catheter, tracheostomy). Acute medical treatments (hospital stays, GP, chemotherapy) remain funded by Health. We clarify at intake so there’s no overlap or billing error.

Q4 Can your nurses liaise with GPs and specialists?

Answer: Yes. Our RNs communicate directly with treating doctors, allied health, and specialists. This ensures continuity, medication safety, and up-to-date care planning.

Part 4B

Disengaged or Hard-to-Engage Participants

Participants who are hesitant, withdrawn, or resistant to services. Common with trauma histories, mental health conditions, or prior poor experiences.

Q1 My participant refuses to let anyone into the house. How will you support them?

Answer: We respect autonomy. Our staff approach gently, at the participant’s pace, and sometimes start with phone/video check-ins. We may suggest “meet and greet” sessions without obligations to slowly build trust. No supports are forced; choice and control remain central.

Q2 The participant has disengaged from multiple providers. Why would it be different with you?

Answer: We take a trauma-informed, person-centred approach. We adapt supports around what matters to the participant, not just “what’s funded.” We spend time listening, setting achievable small goals, and ensuring staff are a good personality match.

Q3 What if they cancel or avoid supports repeatedly?

Answer: We document cancellations and follow-up with the participant and support coordinator to explore why. Sometimes reducing hours or offering flexible supports helps. We avoid penalty fees in genuine cases of disengagement unless agreed upfront in the Service Agreement.

Q4 How do you encourage re-engagement?

Answer: By focusing on participant goals. For example, if independence or social outings motivate them, we tailor supports to those outcomes rather than generic tasks. We also involve trusted family/friends if the participant agrees.

Part 4C

Special Transition Scenarios

Participants moving between stages of life or services (e.g., child to adult, school to community, SIL to independent living).

Q1 Our child is turning 18. How do you support transition to adult services?

Answer: We plan proactively. Supports may change from school-based to community access, vocational skills, and independence building. We involve the young person in decision-making and coordinate with schools, therapists, and families to ensure continuity.

Q2 The participant wants to move from SIL to independent living. How do you help?

Answer: We assess daily living skills, arrange trials with drop-in supports, and liaise with housing providers. Supports are gradually “stepped down” from 24/7 SIL to part-time supports, giving the participant confidence to live independently while staying safe.

Q3 Do you support transition planning for school leavers?

Answer: Yes. We assist with School Leaver Employment Supports (SLES), community access, and skill-building. We coordinate with support coordinators and schools to ensure funding is directed toward training, work experience, and independence goals.

Part 4D

Cultural & Spiritual Considerations

Special scenarios where cultural practices, spiritual beliefs, or community networks are central to intake.

Q1 The participant needs staff from a specific cultural background. Can you provide this?

Answer: Where possible, yes. We record language, cultural, and religious preferences at intake. We aim to match staff accordingly, but if unavailable, we provide cultural training and interpreters to respect those needs.

Q2 Will you respect religious practices (prayer times, fasting, dietary needs)?

Answer: Absolutely. We ask about these preferences at intake and ensure they are integrated into the care plan. Staff receive cultural competence training to support participants’ spiritual wellbeing.

Part 5A

Transition from Another Provider

For participants currently with another provider who wish to change due to dissatisfaction, relocation, or new needs.

Q1 Do I need to give a reason for switching providers?

Answer: No. Participants have the right to choose their provider at any time. Many share their reasons with us to help tailor supports, but it’s entirely your choice. We respect your decision without judgement.

Q2 What if my current provider refuses to release records?

Answer: Your current provider is required to cooperate in safe transition of care. If they delay, we can still commence services using your Service Agreement and intake information, then integrate records once received. You can also involve your Support Coordinator for advocacy if needed.

Q3 Will there be gaps in services when switching?

Answer: We aim for seamless transition. We confirm end date with the old provider and start immediately after, so there’s no gap. For essential supports, we prioritise continuity and communicate closely with all parties involved.

Q4 How do service bookings transfer in the NDIS system?

Answer: If your plan is Agency-managed, the old provider must end their booking. Then we create a new service booking. If Plan- or Self-managed, we can start directly once you sign a Service Agreement. We guide you through the process step by step.

Q5 Will I have to pay exit fees to my current provider?

Answer: Some providers include short notice or exit clauses in their Service Agreements. We advise reviewing your current contract. If there’s a dispute, you may escalate to the NDIS Commission. We never charge exit fees ourselves.

Q6 Will new staff know my routines?

Answer: Yes. We conduct a thorough intake interview and, with your consent, review notes from your previous provider. This ensures staff understand your preferences, routines, and support needs from day one.

Part 5B

Plan Reviews & Funding Changes

Helping participants prepare for NDIS plan reviews, adapt to new budgets, and secure funding aligned with goals.

Q1 Will you help me prepare for my plan review?

Answer: Yes. We provide detailed progress reports that outline services delivered, goals achieved, and any additional supports required. Reports are written in NDIS language to clearly demonstrate outcomes and justify funding needs.

Q2 What happens if I underspent my plan?

Answer: If underspending occurs, we explain why (e.g., hospital admission, participant choice). This helps avoid reduced budgets in the future. We also suggest strategies to use funds more effectively, such as capacity-building supports.

Q3 What happens if I overspent my plan?

Answer: We notify you early if spending is tracking high. If overspend occurs, we provide evidence for why (e.g., increased needs, emergencies). This supports requests for higher budgets at review. We never bill beyond available funding without prior consent.

Q4 Do you attend review meetings with the NDIA?

Answer: Providers are not usually invited unless requested by the participant. If you would like us present, we’re happy to attend or provide a written statement of support. The final decision is always yours.

Q5 How do you document outcomes for plan reviews?

Answer: We track progress against your NDIS goals and include specific evidence (e.g., “Participant now attends community activities twice weekly independently”). We also highlight unmet needs requiring additional funding. Our reports align with NDIA expectations for measurable outcomes.

Q6 What if my plan is cut and I can’t afford the same supports?

Answer: We help you prioritise essential supports within the budget and advocate (with your consent) for a review or appeal. We provide evidence of impact to support requests for funding adjustments.

Q7 Do you work with Support Coordinators on reviews?

Answer: Yes. We collaborate closely with SCs to ensure consistent information is presented to the NDIA. We share reports, spending summaries, and recommendations so the participant receives strong, unified advocacy.

Part 6A

Aged Care & Older Participants

For participants close to or over 65 years, navigating overlaps between NDIS and Aged Care systems.

Q1 I’m turning 65 soon. Will I lose my NDIS plan?

Answer: No. If you entered NDIS before turning 65, you stay with NDIS for life (unless you choose My Aged Care instead). Your plan continues with reviews as normal. Only new applicants after 65 go through the aged care system.

Q2 What if I prefer My Aged Care over NDIS?

Answer: That’s your choice. My Aged Care offers Home Care Packages, but usually with lower funding than NDIS. If you already have an NDIS plan, most participants choose to remain with NDIS because of broader supports and funding flexibility.

Q3 Can you provide both NDIS and aged care supports together?

Answer: Yes. Some participants have both. We ensure clear separation of billing streams (NDIS vs My Aged Care) to stay compliant. For example, personal care may be under NDIS, while domestic tasks may be My Aged Care funded.

Q4 Do you provide nursing and aged care-style supports?

Answer: Yes. Our team includes RNs and carers experienced in aged care. We provide assistance with ADLs, continence, mobility, medication, and end-of-life care. Supports are tailored to maintain independence, dignity, and safety at home.

Q5 How do you handle dementia or age-related decline?

Answer: We follow dementia care best practices—consistent staff, low-stimulus environments, meaningful engagement, and safety measures. We adapt care plans as cognition changes and support families with education and respite options.

Part 6B

Children & Paediatric Participants

For families with children receiving NDIS, focusing on development, therapy, respite, and parental concerns.

Q1 My child is only 4. Are they eligible for NDIS?

Answer: Yes, if your child has a permanent and significant disability, or developmental delay. Early Childhood Approach (ECA) providers support children under 9 with NDIS-funded early interventions. We guide parents through this process.

Q2 Do you provide therapy supports for children?

Answer: Yes. We link families with occupational therapists, speech pathologists, psychologists, and physiotherapists. We also provide therapy assistants who help children practice skills at home or in the community.

Q3 What about school support?

Answer: While schools are responsible for curriculum support, NDIS can fund personal care, therapy, or transport around schooling. We work with schools to ensure children are safe, supported, and engaged in learning.

Q4 Do you provide respite for parents?

Answer: Yes. Respite can be in-home, community-based, or Short-Term Accommodation (STA). It provides children with fun, engaging care while giving parents vital rest. Activities are tailored to each child’s interests and needs.

Q5 My child has challenging behaviours. How will staff manage this?

Answer: Staff follow Behaviour Support Plans developed by specialists. Strategies include positive reinforcement, structured routines, and low-stress environments. Safety and wellbeing are prioritised while supporting skill-building.

Q6 Will staff respect our parenting style?

Answer: Absolutely. Parents are the primary decision-makers. We align supports with family values, routines, and cultural preferences, ensuring children feel secure and parents feel respected.

Q7 How do you ensure child safety?

Answer: All staff hold NDIS Worker Screening and Working with Children Checks. Safeguards include supervision policies, incident reporting, and clear communication with parents. We also maintain strict compliance with the NDIS Child Safe Standards.

Part 7A

Crisis & Emergency Scenarios

When participants, families, or staff face an emergency or crisis situation during intake or ongoing supports.

Q1 What if the participant experiences a mental health crisis?

Answer: Our staff are trained in mental health first aid and de-escalation. If there’s immediate danger, we call 000. We then notify family and the participant’s treating team. We document the incident and review strategies with professionals for ongoing safety.

Q2 How do you handle a medical emergency at home?

Answer: Staff follow emergency care plans first (e.g., seizure protocols). If urgent, they call 000, administer first aid, and stay with the participant until help arrives. We notify family immediately, complete incident reports, and escalate to management the same day.

Q3 What if there are safeguarding concerns (abuse, neglect, exploitation)?

Answer: We follow strict safeguarding protocols. Any suspicion is documented and escalated to management, the NDIS Commission, and/or relevant authorities as required. Staff are mandated reporters. Participant safety and dignity are always the priority.

Q4 Do you provide crisis respite if a family can’t cope?

Answer: Yes. Subject to staff availability, we provide urgent respite at home or Short-Term Accommodation (STA). This ensures the participant is safe while giving families immediate relief. We then review longer-term strategies to avoid repeat crises.

Q5 How do you prevent re-occurring crises?

Answer: We work with participants, families, and professionals to create proactive crisis plans. These include early warning signs, escalation contacts, preferred calming techniques, and pre-approved emergency responses.

Part 7B

Complaints & Escalations

Participants or families may raise urgent issues, disputes, or dissatisfaction with services.

Q1 What if I have a complaint about staff or services?

Answer: We encourage open communication. Complaints can be made verbally, in writing, or anonymously. All complaints are acknowledged within 24 hours, investigated, and outcomes shared with you. If unsatisfied, you may escalate directly to the NDIS Quality & Safeguards Commission.

Q2 Will I be penalised for complaining?

Answer: Absolutely not. Feedback and complaints help us improve. Participants will never be penalised for raising concerns—services will continue unaffected. We welcome feedback as part of our quality improvement culture.

Q3 How fast are complaints resolved?

Answer: Minor issues are often resolved within 48 hours. Complex matters may take up to 21 days. We provide regular updates and keep you informed throughout the process.

Part 7C

Interpreter & Non-English Speaker Support

For participants or families who speak little or no English, ensuring communication is clear and culturally respectful.

Q1 My first language isn’t English. Can you provide interpreters?

Answer: Yes. We arrange accredited interpreters (phone, video, or face-to-face) through the National Translating and Interpreting Service (TIS). This ensures participants and families fully understand intake and service planning.

Q2 Do you have bilingual staff?

Answer: Where possible, yes. We record language preferences at intake and match staff accordingly. If bilingual staff aren’t available, interpreters are engaged to ensure communication is clear.

Q3 How do you make written information accessible?

Answer: We can provide Service Agreements, intake forms, and key policies in translated versions where available. For less common languages, we read documents aloud with an interpreter to ensure full understanding before signing.

Q4 Do you respect cultural practices alongside language needs?

Answer: Yes. Staff receive cultural awareness training. We incorporate dietary, religious, and cultural needs into the care plan and respect participants’ spiritual and community connections as part of holistic care.

Part 8A

Handling Objections — Model Responses

Use calm, clear, and respectful phrasing. Acknowledge, clarify, answer, and check comfort before moving on.

O1 “It’s too expensive / I don’t want out-of-pocket costs.”

Model: “Totally understand wanting no surprises. We bill strictly to your NDIS plan at the standard Price Guide rates. Before we start, we’ll list the exact line items and expected hours in the Service Agreement so you can approve or change anything first.”

Offer to send a one-page quote with weekly cost and remaining budget forecast.

O2 “Can you start tomorrow?”

Model: “I’ll check roster availability immediately. If we can’t place for tomorrow, we’ll prioritise you for the earliest slot and update you today with a confirmed start time and interim options if needed.”

  • Ask urgency drivers (discharge, safety risk, care gap).
  • Offer a nurse/short shift bridge if applicable.
O3 “Staff didn’t show last time with another provider.”

Model: “I’m sorry that happened. We run proactive confirmations and backups. If someone is unwell, we notify you early and, with your permission, send a qualified replacement. You’ll always know who is coming and when.”

Explain SMS confirmations, live roster changes, and on-call number.

O4 “We prefer a female/male worker / a specific culture or language.”

Model: “No problem—your comfort matters. We’ll prioritise that preference. If we can’t match perfectly at first, we’ll discuss interim options and keep searching until you’re happy.”

O5 “What if I don’t like the worker?”

Model: “You have full choice. Tell us and we’ll rematch quickly—no hard feelings. We want you to feel comfortable and supported.”

O6 “Can you do cash rates / discount below NDIS price?”

Model: “We follow NDIS Pricing Arrangements for fairness and compliance. What we can do is plan supports efficiently to get the most value out of your budget.”

O7 “Do you charge travel?”

Model: “Only as allowed by NDIS (time and km caps). If travel applies to your location, we’ll show it in the Agreement upfront so there are no surprises.”

O8 “We need after-hours/weekends but heard rates are higher.”

Model: “Correct—NDIS has different rates for evenings, weekends, and public holidays. We’ll map your ideal schedule and show both standard and after-hours costs so you can choose what suits.”

O9 “We only want private carers, not a provider.”

Model: “Totally your choice. The benefit of a provider is vetted staff, replacements, insurance cover, clinical oversight, and 24/7 support line. If you prefer self-recruited workers, we can still help with nursing or backup coverage when needed.”

O10 “We’re worried about safety/abuse.”

Model: “Safety is non-negotiable. All workers hold NDIS Worker Screening (and WWCC where required), complete safeguarding training, and follow strict incident/complaints policies. You can contact management anytime.”

O11 “I don’t want to lock into a contract.”

Model: “Our Service Agreement is plain English and flexible. It protects your rights and explains rates, cancellations, and changes. You can end services with notice—usually 14 days.”

O12 “How do I know you’re qualified?”

Model: “We’re an NDIS Registered provider and audited externally. Staff complete competencies (manual handling, medications, high-intensity tasks) and ongoing training. We’re happy to share credentials relevant to your supports.”

O13 “Can you guarantee the same worker every time?”

Model: “We build a small, consistent team so you usually see the same faces. If someone is away, we’ll brief a backup and introduce them in advance.”

O14 “We’ve had poor communication before.”

Model: “We set clear contact channels—SMS confirmations, a dedicated inbox/phone, and escalation to a team lead. We also agree on update frequency (weekly/fortnightly) so you’re never left guessing.”

O15 “What if things go wrong?”

Model: “Tell us immediately—no matter how small. We log the issue, fix it quickly, and follow up to make sure you’re satisfied. You can also complain formally or to the NDIS Commission at any time.”

O16 “Do you do trial shifts?”

Model: “Yes—many participants like a short trial. We schedule a meet-and-greet or first short visit; if you’re happy, we lock in the regular roster.”

O17 “What’s your cancellation policy?”

Model: “We align with NDIS cancellation rules (typically 7 clear days). If circumstances are outside your control, talk to us—we do our best to be fair.”

O18 “Can you provide references/testimonials?”

Model: “Yes—on request we can share de-identified feedback and, where appropriate, referee contacts. We also offer a short pilot period so you can try us first.”

O19 “We’re confused about NDIS rules.”

Model: “No worries—NDIS can be complex. We’ll explain funding categories, how claiming works, and what’s reasonable & necessary for your goals—always in plain English.”

O20 “Do you have minimum hours?”

Model: “Some visits have a short minimum to cover travel/setup. We’ll outline it upfront and design shifts that make sense for you and your budget.”

Part 8B

Follow-ups & Call Closures

Finish every call with clarity, reassurance, and written confirmation.

C1 How do you wrap up the call professionally?

Model: “Thanks for sharing today. I’ll send a summary email/SMS now with the agreed next steps. If anything changes, message or call us anytime.”

  • Confirm who will send the follow-up and when (e.g., within 1 hour).
  • List attachments: quote, draft Service Agreement, intake form link.
  • Set a check-in time (e.g., “I’ll call Thursday 10am”).
C2 What goes into the written follow-up?
  • Concise recap of needs/goals and preferred schedule.
  • Proposed start date + who’s doing what (you/us/SC).
  • Pricing table (line item, hourly rate, est. weekly hours).
  • Links: intake form, Service Agreement, contact details, on-call.

Keep it one screen long; attach detail as PDFs if needed.

C3 How do you keep momentum without pressure?

Model: “No rush—when you’re ready, reply ‘YES’ to this SMS or sign the Agreement, and we’ll lock in your start and staff matching.”

C4 What if we’re waiting on documents/capacity?

Model: “I’ll update you by [time/date]. If anything shifts sooner, I’ll message right away. You’ll never be left wondering.”

Part 9A

Intake Call Documentation

Accurate, objective, and audit-ready notes are essential for compliance and safe handover.

Q1 What must be captured in intake notes?
  • Caller Details: name, role/relationship, contact, consent to SMS/email.
  • Participant Info: legal/preferred name, DOB, disability/diagnoses, suburb/postcode.
  • NDIS Plan: plan number, management type, dates, funding categories.
  • Goals & Priorities: what the participant wants to achieve.
  • Service Preferences: days/times, gender/staff preferences, cultural/linguistic needs.
  • Risks/Alerts: behaviours of concern, allergies, equipment needs.
  • Consent: verbal consent scope recorded (sharing info, SC/plan manager comms).
  • Outcome: next steps, timelines, who will follow up.
Q2 How should notes be written?

Answer: Notes must be factual, clear, and free of bias. Use the participant’s words where possible.

Good: “Caller reports missed medication twice this week.”

Poor: “Family unreliable.” (judgemental)

Part 9B

Consent & Risk Flags

Always check permission, privacy boundaries, and safety risks.

Q1 What consent needs recording?
  • Consent for storing and sharing personal information.
  • Consent to liaise with support coordinators, plan managers, or family.
  • Consent to send SMS/email communications.
  • Interpreter and cultural needs recorded.
Q2 How do you capture risk alerts?

Answer: Document specific risks (e.g., “Seizure history — midazolam prescribed, staff to be trained”). Avoid vague terms (“High risk”). Include triggers, early warning signs, and required responses.

Part 9C

Compliance & Quality KPIs

Measure intake quality against NDIS and internal standards.

Q1 What are the key compliance metrics?
  • First Response Time: calls answered or returned within agreed window.
  • Conversion Rate: enquiries to signed Service Agreements.
  • Time to First Service: days from intake to first shift.
  • Documentation Completeness: % of notes with all required fields.
  • Risk Escalation: % of flagged issues escalated correctly.
  • Participant Satisfaction: follow-up survey scores after first week.
Q2 What happens if compliance fails?

Answer: Non-compliance can result in audit findings, funding delays, or risk to participant safety. Immediate corrective action, retraining, and documentation audits are required if gaps are found.

Part 10A

Governance & Roles

Clear ownership ensures the scripts stay current, compliant, and effective.

  • Content Owner (Intake Lead): updates scripts quarterly, integrates staff feedback.
  • Clinical Owner (RN Lead): reviews clinical accuracy, risk management, and safeguarding.
  • Quality Manager: audits call notes, monitors KPIs, and conducts calibrations.
  • Training Manager: ensures onboarding and refresher training uses this script.
  • IT/CRM Admin: maintains digital accessibility and integration into systems.
Part 10B

Training & Rollout

Introduce scripts in a structured rollout to maximise adoption.

  1. Phase 1 — Induction: All new intake staff complete guided training using sample calls.
  2. Phase 2 — Role-play: Staff simulate scenarios (general, crisis, complaints, objections).
  3. Phase 3 — Supervised Calls: Shadowing by senior intake staff until confident.
  4. Phase 4 — Live Monitoring: Quality manager reviews first 10 calls for compliance.
  5. Refresher Training: Quarterly sessions and after any major script update.
Part 10C

Monitoring & Scorecards

Measure ongoing performance against participant experience and compliance standards.

  • Call Calibration: monthly review of random calls with scoring rubric (tone, accuracy, documentation).
  • Audit Logs: all calls linked with notes checked against compliance dataset.
  • Participant Feedback: SMS/email surveys post-intake asking clarity & satisfaction.
  • Scorecard Metrics: empathy, accuracy, compliance, resolution, timeliness.
Part 10D

Continuous Improvement

Keep the scripts alive — updated with reality, policy changes, and feedback.

  • Monthly: review common objections & update model responses.
  • Quarterly: update training guide with new NDIS policy changes or price guide updates.
  • Annual: full review & re-audit; refresh staff training and re-certify compliance.
  • Feedback Loop: encourage staff to submit “real call” examples to refine scripts.
  • Version Control: assign version numbers (v1.1, v1.2) and keep archive for audits.
Part 10E

Conclusion & Next Steps

Bring it all together — empower staff and safeguard participants.

  • Always lead with empathy and active listening.
  • Provide clear, accurate, and jargon-free responses.
  • Respect participant choice, culture, and autonomy at all times.
  • Follow through with commitments and keep participants informed.
  • Document every call objectively — compliance protects both participant and provider.

Thank you for committing to best-practice intake. This work ensures participants feel safe, respected, and supported from the very first phone call.
