Write notes that are clear, objective, and audit-ready. Capture only what you need, but capture it accurately.

Principles

  • Objective, not judgemental: use observed facts and caller quotes (verbatim in quotation marks).
  • Complete, not cluttered: capture required fields first; add context only if it changes decisions/risks.
  • Plain English: avoid jargon; expand acronyms on first use.
  • Privacy by default: record necessary personal info only; no gossip or speculation.
  • Timestamp everything: date/time, who you spoke with, your name/role.

Required fields (minimum dataset)

  • Caller: name, role/relationship, contact, OK to SMS (Y/N).

  • Goals/priorities; preferred days/times; worker preferences (gender/language/culture).
  • Existing supports/providers; reason for change if transitioning.
  • Risks/alerts (behaviours, seizures, falls, allergies, pressure injury risk); equipment/AT.
  • Consent scope recorded (who can receive updates); interpreter required (Y/N & language).
