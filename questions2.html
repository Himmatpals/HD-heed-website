Universal Standards (use on every call)

Welcome to the audio lesson on Universal Standards. This elaborative lesson will teach you the standards to apply on every call, including tone, speed, structure, compliance, and empathy. Listen to the guidelines, golden lines, and practice mirroring the language to build rapport with callers.

  • Tone Warm, unhurried, person-centred. Avoid jargon unless the caller uses it. Mirror their language gently.
  • Speed 140–160 wpm. Pause to check understanding.
  • Structure Greet → Purpose → Permission → Discovery → Offer → Next steps → Confirm.
  • Compliance Privacy, consent, and accurate notes every time (NDIS-aligned). If in doubt, capture & escalate.
  • Empathy Validate feelings; acknowledge effort; focus on goals and outcomes.
Golden line: “Thanks for calling HD HeeD — how can we make things easier for you today?”
“I’m here with you.” • “Take your time.” • “I’ll summarise back to make sure I’ve got it right.”

General Intake Script (default)

Welcome to the audio lesson on the General Intake Script. This lesson will teach you the default script for most inbound calls, including greeting, consent, discovery, and closing. Listen to the phrasing and practice delivering it unhurriedly to guide the caller effectively.

Use for most inbound calls 
👋 “Good <time-of-day>, you’ve reached HD HeeD, this is <name>. How can I help today?”
🔐 “Before we begin, is it okay if I take a few details so we can support you properly?”
🧭 “What would you like help with right now?” (Listen. Reflect back a short summary.)
✅ “We can help with <service>. We’re NDIS registered and clinically led. The next step is to understand your goals and supports, then line up the right team.”
🧾 “Can I grab your full name, best contact, suburb, and NDIS number (if handy)?”
🧩 Discovery (see Data Capture). Check safety, timeframes, preferences.
📌 “Here’s what I can do for you today: <action>. We can start <when>.”
🗓 “Would you like me to email or text a summary with next steps?”
🤝 “Thanks for calling — you’re in good hands. Anything else I can answer?”

Data Capture Checklist

Welcome to the audio lesson on Data Capture Checklist. This lesson will teach you the essential details to capture during calls, including participant info, NDIS details, goals, and risks. Listen to the checklist and practice confirming spellings and repeating numbers for accuracy.

  • Participant legal name; preferred name; DOB; pronouns (optional).
  • Best phone & email; OK to SMS?
  • Suburb & postcode; living situation; access constraints at home.
  • NDIS number; plan dates; plan manager (or self/agency managed); budget category(s) & capacity.
  • Primary goals; priorities; days/times preferred; cultural or language needs; interpreter required.
  • Existing supports; current provider(s); reason for change if transitioning.
  • Risks/alerts (falls, behaviours, pressure injuries, seizures, allergies); equipment/AT.

Tip: confirm spelling aloud for names/suburbs; repeat NDIS number in pairs.

Consent & Privacy Lines

Welcome to the audio lesson on Consent & Privacy Lines. This lesson will teach you how to obtain and record consent using friendly phrasing. Listen to the lines for different situations and practice explaining privacy to build trust.

Use matter-of-fact, friendly phrasing. Obtain consent before collecting or sharing personal info.

Collecting details (participant on the line)
Line: “I’ll note your details to arrange supports. We keep your information private and use it only for care. Is that okay to proceed?”
Third party (family/SC) — verbal consent
Line: “Do we have <participant’s name> on the line to confirm they’re happy for you to speak on their behalf? If not, I can take general info now and organise a quick consent call or consent form.”
Sharing updates with referrers
Line: “Would you like us to share progress updates with <name>? We’ll only share what’s needed to coordinate your supports.”
Recording consent in notes
• Verbal consent: Y/N, who gave it, date/time
• Scope: intake only / ongoing updates / full coordination
• Review/expiry date for consent

FAQs — Perfect Answers (Full Set)

Welcome to the audio lesson on FAQs — Perfect Answers. This lesson will teach you how to respond to common questions with confidence and clarity. Listen to each question and perfect answer, and practice delivering them professionally to reassure callers.

Are you NDIS registered and what services do you offer?
Yes — HD HeeD is an NDIS registered provider. We support daily personal activities, domestic assistance, community participation, skill building, in-home respite, and clinically led nursing care at home. Share your goals and suburb; I’ll confirm what we can start and when.
How soon can services start?
Standard supports often begin within days once we confirm details and the Service Agreement. Complex/nursing supports may require a brief clinician review first. I’ll give you the earliest realistic start window now and watch for any earlier openings.
How do payments work for NDIA-managed, plan-managed, and self-managed?
• NDIA-managed: we create a service booking and claim directly — no out-of-pocket.
• Plan-managed: we invoice your Plan Manager.
• Self-managed: we invoice you; you then claim from the NDIS portal. Our rates follow the NDIS Pricing Arrangements.
What about travel, public holidays, or short-notice cancellations?
We follow NDIS rules. Any allowable travel or public holiday loadings are listed upfront in your Service Agreement. We aim to reschedule where possible to avoid cancellation fees.
Will I get the same worker each time?
We prioritise continuity and build a small, consistent team. We’ll also set a backup so there’s coverage for leave or illness, with advance notice whenever possible.
Can I choose worker gender, language, or cultural background?
We’ll do our best to match your preferences while keeping safety and quality front-of-mind. Tell me what matters to you and I’ll note it for rostering.
Do you talk to my Support Coordinator or Plan Manager?
Yes — with your consent we coordinate with your Support Coordinator and Plan Manager so invoicing and service bookings are smooth and aligned to your goals.
I don’t know my NDIS number — can we still proceed?
Absolutely. We can start with your contact details and suburb. Your NDIS number helps link your plan later — we can confirm it with you or your SC/PM.
Can you help with reports for a plan review?
Yes. We can summarise supports delivered, progress toward goals, and any unmet needs, and provide notes for your review meeting — with your consent.
What happens in an emergency or after hours?
If there’s danger or a medical emergency, call 000. For urgent non-emergencies, we follow our escalation pathway and place priority notes for the on-call/next business day team. We’ll agree on your preferred contact method during intake.

Time-sensitive coordination

Perfect answers (common objections)

“Can you start within 48 hours?”
“We’ll prioritise your case. If helpful, we can connect with the ward pre-discharge for a direct handover and schedule day-one supports. I’ll confirm exact times today.”
“AT/home modifications aren’t ready.”
“We’ll implement safe interim strategies (extra staffing/alternate techniques) and coordinate with OT/supplier on delivery dates.”
“We’ll ramp down after week one.”

Day 0–7 checklist

  • RN assessment within 24–48h; vitals & wound check if applicable.
  • Medication chart set-up; explain red-flag symptoms & who to call.
  • Home safety walk-through; emergency numbers visible.
  • Follow-up visits booked; GP review coordinated if advised.

Crisis / Immediate Risk

Welcome to the audio lesson on Crisis / Immediate Risk. This lesson will teach you the emergency pathway for urgent calls. Listen to the script and practice staying on the line while escalating to ensure safety.

Emergency pathway 
🚨 “Are you or anyone with you in immediate danger, or without essential care today?”
👉 If YES: stay on the line, call 000, alert internal clinical lead, document exact words/time.

What to listen for

Perfect answers — FAQs

“Can you send someone right now?”
“If safe and within reach, we’ll deploy immediately. If not, we’ll stay with you on the phone and connect you with emergency services.”

Interpreter / CALD (Culturally & Linguistically Diverse)

Welcome to the audio lesson on Interpreter / CALD. This lesson will teach you how to handle language and cultural needs, including arranging interpreters and respecting practices. Listen to the script and practice asking for preferences to ensure respectful support.

Language access & cultural respect 
🌏 “Which language would you prefer for our calls and visits?”
📞 “We can arrange a professional interpreter (phone or face-to-face).”
🙏 “Are there cultural or religious practices you’d like us to respect in how we deliver support?”
📌 “We’ll record your preferences and make sure every staff member is briefed before visits.”

Perfect answers — FAQs

“Can my family interpret?”
“They can if you prefer, but for accuracy and privacy we recommend a professional interpreter. We can arrange that at no cost to you.”
“How do I request the same interpreter each time?”
“We’ll request consistency, though it depends on interpreter availability. We’ll note your preference and book early where possible.”
“Do workers speak my language?”
“We’ll check our team for language matches. If not, we’ll pair workers with interpreters and still respect cultural preferences.”

Cultural sensitivity checklist

  • Always ask preferred language first — don’t assume from name/accent.
  • Ask if the participant prefers same-gender workers for personal care.
  • Check for cultural/religious routines (dietary, prayer times, modesty).
  • Respect decision-making style (individual vs family-centred).
  • Use plain language; avoid jargon; pause to check understanding.

Interpreter logistics

Phone interpreter (TIS National)
• Dial 131 450 (24/7).
• Provide language needed, participant’s suburb, and your provider ID.
• Note interpreter code for continuity if the participant prefers.
Face-to-face interpreter
• Request 3–5 days ahead if possible.
• Confirm time and location clearly; allow extra time for discussion.
• Always introduce interpreter, explain their role, and confirm confidentiality.
NDIS compliance note
• Record that an interpreter was offered and accepted/declined.
• Note interpreter’s name/code and type (phone / face-to-face).
• Document any cultural preferences flagged and how they’ll be respected.
Golden line: “Your language and culture matter. We’ll make sure support feels respectful and clear — always in the way that suits you best.”

Handling Objections & Model Responses

Welcome to the audio lesson on Handling Objections & Model Responses. This lesson will teach you how to address common concerns in intake calls. Listen to each objection and model response, and practice acknowledging feelings to turn objections into trust-building opportunities.

Intake often means facing concerns, doubts, or objections. Below are the most common ones with model professional responses. Each answer is designed to reassure, clarify, and move the intake forward without pressure.

Funding & Costs

“I don’t want to pay anything out-of-pocket.”
“Absolutely — our supports are billed directly to your NDIS plan if they’re reasonable and necessary. We’ll confirm line items with you before starting so there are no surprises.”
“Why is your rate higher than another provider?”
“All our rates are within the NDIS Pricing Arrangements. If you’ve seen a different rate, it may be because that provider isn’t including travel, public holiday loadings, or is using a different line item. I can walk you through a transparent comparison.”
“What if my plan doesn’t have enough funds?”
“We’ll check your plan budget with you and help prioritise supports. We can also work with your Support Coordinator or Plan Manager to explore flexibility or request a plan reassessment if needed.”

Trust & Reliability

“Last time, staff didn’t show up.”
“I’m sorry to hear that. We guarantee communication — if a worker is unwell, we notify you early and send a backup if you want. We keep rosters stable so you know who’s coming and when.”
“I don’t want too many different workers.”
“We’ll keep your team small — usually a primary worker and one backup. We’ll ask your preference first and make sure you’re comfortable.”
“I had a bad experience with another provider.”
“I understand that must have been hard. We’ll go at your pace, start with a short trial, and check in with you after the first week. You’ll have direct contact if anything feels wrong.”

Timing & Availability

“Can you start tomorrow?”
“I’ll check our local availability right now. If we can’t place a worker tomorrow, we’ll prioritise you for the soonest slot and keep you updated as shifts open.”
“Why is there a waitlist?”
“We match the right staff to your needs — especially if you’ve requested specific skills or cultural matches. While waiting, we can keep you updated and sometimes arrange interim supports.”
“What if I only want support for 2 hours a week?”
“That’s fine — supports can be as little or as much as your plan and goals allow. We’ll schedule what you’re comfortable with.”

Scope of Services

“Do you provide housing too?”
“We don’t provide housing directly, but we can support with daily living, tenancy support, and link you to housing services if needed.”
“Can you do transport?”
“Yes — transport to appointments, community activities, and shopping is included if it’s in your plan. We’ll confirm the right line item and keep a log of trips.”
“Do you do therapy?”
“We provide RN-led clinical care but not allied health therapy. We can collaborate with your therapists and follow their plans if you’d like.”

Consent & Privacy

“I don’t want my family involved.”
“That’s completely your choice. We’ll only involve people you authorise, and we’ll note your preferences clearly so staff know who to share updates with.”
“Can I change my mind about who gets updates?”
“Yes — you can update your consent at any time. We’ll make sure it’s recorded and followed straight away.”
“How is my information stored?”
“All information is stored securely under NDIS rules and Australian privacy law. Access is only for staff directly supporting your care.”

Clinical & Safety

“Your staff won’t know my medical needs.”
“We’ll arrange an RN assessment and detailed care plan first. Workers receive training and written instructions before starting.”
“What if something goes wrong?”
“We have clear escalation steps: staff contact the on-call clinician or 000 depending on urgency, and we notify your GP and family if you wish. All incidents are documented and reviewed.”
“Do you do restrictive practices?”
“Only if authorised under a Behaviour Support Plan by an NDIS Behaviour Support Practitioner, and only with your consent and oversight.”
Golden rule: Always acknowledge the concern, show empathy, provide a clear factual answer, and outline next steps. Never dismiss or rush objections — they’re opportunities to build trust.

Documentation Templates

Welcome to the audio lesson on Documentation Templates. This lesson will teach you how to write clear, objective notes for audits. Listen to the principles and templates, and practice using SBAR or SOAP for accurate records.

Write notes that are clear, objective, and audit-ready. Capture only what you need, but capture it accurately.

Principles

  • Objective, not judgemental: use observed facts and caller quotes (verbatim in quotation marks).
  • Complete, not cluttered: capture required fields first; add context only if it changes decisions/risks.
  • Plain English: avoid jargon; expand acronyms on first use.
  • Privacy by default: record necessary personal info only; no gossip or speculation.
  • Timestamp everything: date/time, who you spoke with, your name/role.

Required fields (minimum dataset)

  • Caller: name, role/relationship, contact, OK to SMS (Y/N).
  • Participant: legal name, preferred name, DOB, suburb/postcode.
  • NDIS details: number (if known), plan dates, management type (NDIA/Plan/Self), budgets known/unknown.
  • Goals/priorities; preferred days/times; worker preferences (gender/language/culture).
  • Existing supports/providers; reason for change if transitioning.
  • Risks/alerts (behaviours, seizures, falls, allergies, pressure injury risk); equipment/AT.
  • Consent scope recorded (who can receive updates); interpreter required (Y/N & language).
  • Outcome & next steps (who, what, when), agreed contact method, follow-up date/time.

Note templates (copy-ready)

SBAR 
SITUATION — Intake call with <Caller Name> (role: <SC/Family/Participant>) about <Participant> on <DD/MM/YYYY> <HH:MM>.
BACKGROUND — NDIS mgmt: <NDIA/Plan/Self>; Plan dates: <start–end>; Suburb: <...>. Existing supports: <...>.
ASSESSMENT — Goals/priorities: <...>. Risks/alerts: <...>. Preferences: <gender/language/schedule>. Interpreter: <Y/N & language>. Consent: <scope>.
RECOMMENDATION/NEXT — Actions: <what> by <who> by <when>. Start window offered: <...>. Follow-up booked: <date/time> via <channel>.
SOAP 
SUBJECTIVE — Caller states: “<verbatim concerns/needs>”.
OBJECTIVE — Participant details verified; NDIS mgmt & plan dates noted; risks/alerts listed; supports/providers documented.
ASSESSMENT — Fit to service: <yes/conditional>; capacity & start window: <...>; clinical review required: <Y/N>.
PLAN — <Action 1> by <owner> due <date/time>; <Action 2> ...; follow-up <date/time> via <channel>.
Narrative (NDIS audit-ready) 
Call with <Caller> (role: <...>). Consented to note details and to share updates with <names> (scope: <...>).
Goals/priorities: <...>. Preferred schedule: <days/times>. Preferences: <gender/language/culture>.
Risks/alerts: <...> (source: caller report). Safety today: <no/yes — actions>.
Offered services: <...> with start window <...>. Agreed next steps: <...>. Follow-up booked for <date/time> by <channel>.
Note by <Your Name>, <Role>, on <DD/MM/YYYY HH:MM>.

Consent logging (matrix)

  • Verbal consent: Y/N; who gave it; date/time; scope (intake only / ongoing updates / full coordination).
  • Third-party authority: Y/N; participant present Y/N; callback arranged for direct consent Y/N.
  • Interpreter offered: accepted/declined; language; interpreter code (if known).
  • Review date for consent; how to withdraw consent (note provided to caller).

Good vs poor notes (examples)

Good
  • “Caller reports ‘missed morning meds twice this week’.”
  • “Plan-managed; plan ends 14/12/2025; budget sufficiency unknown.”
  • “Start window offered: next Tue–Thu mornings; meet-and-greet first.”
  • “Escalated to RN for wound review; action due 28/09 10:00.”
Poor
  • “Family unreliable.” (judgemental; offer facts instead)
  • “Lots of issues.” (vague; specify what/when/impact)
  • No timestamps, no owner for actions, no consent record.

Privacy & data minimisation

  • Store only necessary personal and clinical details; avoid unrelated history.
  • Never include credentials (portal passwords) or unnecessary third-party info.
  • Mark sensitive risk details clearly; restrict visibility as per role-based access.
  • If notes contain allegations or safeguarding concerns, flag and follow escalation (see below).
  • Record corrections/amendments with timestamp and author; never delete audit trails.

Quality & KPIs

Welcome to the audio lesson on Quality & KPIs. This lesson will teach you the framework for measuring call quality and performance. Listen to the KPIs and practice tracking your own metrics for continuous improvement.

Core KPIs (targets)

  • First Response Time (FRT) — call answered or callback within agreed window (e.g., same business day for new enquiries).
  • Time to First Service (TTFS) — days from intake to first shift/visit; tracked by scenario (standard, nursing, discharge).
  • Conversion Rate — enquiries → service agreements (or booked first shifts).
  • Consent Accuracy — % of calls with complete consent fields (scope, who, date/time) recorded.
  • Risk Escalation Compliance — % of red-flag calls escalated correctly and within timeframe.
  • Documentation Completeness — % of notes meeting minimum dataset + one objective quote.
  • Continuity — % of participants with primary + named backup worker in first 14 days.
  • Participant Satisfaction — CSAT/NPS or post-first-week pulse (“How was your first support?”).
  • Rework — % of invoices/quotes returned due to errors (lower is better).
  • Privacy Incidents — target zero; investigate and learn if any occur.

How we measure

  • Weekly report: FRT, TTFS, conversions by scenario and suburb.
  • Monthly QA sample: ≥10% of calls per staff member scored via rubric.
  • Risk audit: 100% of escalations reviewed next business day.
  • Participant pulse: SMS/email after week 1, logged in CRM.
  • Quarterly calibration: team reviews the same 5 calls to sync standards.
Golden line: “If we can’t measure it, we can’t improve it.” — Keep metrics simple, visible, owned.
