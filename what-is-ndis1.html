What Is NDIS and How Can It Help You?

A friendly guide for participants and carers exploring NDIS support options.

Meet Maya, a bright 8-year-old who loves to paint. Maya has a physical disability that makes some daily tasks and joining her friends in activities a bit challenging. Her mum, Priya, does everything she can to support Maya, but it can be overwhelming at times. Then they discovered the NDIS – and everything started to change. With NDIS funding, Maya got a custom wheelchair, regular therapy sessions, and a support worker who helps her attend an art class every week. Maya is blossoming, and her mum finally has some breathing room and hope for the future.

This is just one story of how the NDIS can make a positive difference. Now, let's explore what the NDIS is and how it might help you or your family.

What is the NDIS?

The NDIS is Australia’s National Disability Insurance Scheme – a government-funded program designed to support people with permanent and significant disabilities. In simpler terms, it’s a way to provide individuals (called participants) with funding for services and support they need to live more independently and achieve their goals.

Under the NDIS, eligible participants work with a planner or Local Area Coordinator to develop a personalized plan for “reasonable and necessary” supports. It gives people control and choice over the services they receive and how they’re delivered.

How Does the NDIS Benefit Individuals with Disabilities?

  • Greater Independence: Support like wheelchairs, assistive tech, or workers can make daily life easier.
  • Improved Wellbeing: Therapies and supports can improve mental, physical, and emotional health.
  • Skill Building and Goals: Funding can support job coaching, training, or learning hobbies and passions.
  • Community Participation: Support for joining activities and social groups to stay engaged and connected.

What Services and Supports Does the NDIS Cover?

  • Personal Care – Help with daily living tasks like showering and dressing
  • Therapies – Physiotherapy, OT, speech, psychology etc.
  • Assistive Technology – Wheelchairs, mobility aids, communication devices
  • Home & Vehicle Modifications – Ramps, rails, car conversions
  • Community Access – Attending outings, clubs, events
  • Employment/Education Support – Help with work or learning
  • Respite – Short-term accommodation or in-home support so carers get a break

How Does the NDIS Support Carers?

NDIS indirectly helps carers by giving participants the tools and support to be more independent. It can also include:

  • Respite care so carers can rest or attend to their own needs
  • Support worker funding to ease daily responsibilities
  • Training and counseling for carers to feel confident and supported

How to Start Your NDIS Journey

  1. Check eligibility – Must be under 65, Australian resident, and have permanent disability
  2. Access Request – Contact NDIA and submit required documents
  3. Planning meeting – Discuss your needs and goals
  4. NDIS Plan created – Tailored to your supports and reviewed yearly
  5. Choose providers – Like HD Heed, who can help deliver supports
