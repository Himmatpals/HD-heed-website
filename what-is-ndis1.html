What Is NDIS and How Can It Help You?

A friendly guide for participants and carers exploring NDIS support options.

Meet Maya, a bright 8-year-old who loves to paint. Maya has a physical disability that makes some daily tasks and joining her friends in activities a bit challenging. Her mum, Priya, does everything she can to support Maya, but it can be overwhelming at times. Then they discovered the NDIS – and everything started to change. With NDIS funding, Maya got a custom wheelchair, regular therapy sessions, and a support worker who helps her attend an art class every week. Maya is blossoming, and her mum finally has some breathing room and hope for the future.

This is just one story of how the NDIS can make a positive difference. Now, let's explore what the NDIS is and how it might help you or your family.

What is the NDIS?

The NDIS is Australia’s National Disability Insurance Scheme – a government-funded program designed to support people with permanent and significant disabilities. In simpler terms, it’s a way to provide individuals (called participants) with funding for services and support they need to live more independently and achieve their goals. Think of it as a safety net that empowers you to choose the help you require, whether it's daily personal care, therapy sessions, or special equipment.

Under the NDIS, eligible participants work with an NDIS planner or Local Area Coordinator (LAC) to develop a personalized plan. This plan allocates funding for “reasonable and necessary” supports. Instead of a one-size-fits-all approach, the NDIS focuses on your unique needs, giving you choice and control over how you use your supports. It’s all about making sure you have the help you need to thrive, on your own terms.

How Does the NDIS Benefit Individuals with Disabilities?

The NDIS is a game-changer for many Australians with disabilities. By providing funding and resources, it opens doors to opportunities that might have been out of reach before. Here are some ways it can benefit you or your family member:

  • Greater Independence: With the right supports – like a wheelchair, assistive technology, or a personal support worker – everyday tasks become easier. You can do more on your own and make more of your own choices.
  • Improved Wellbeing: Access to therapies (physio, occupational therapy, counseling, etc.) and support services can improve health and emotional wellbeing. This means a better quality of life and more time to enjoy with family and friends.
  • Skill Building and Goals: Want to learn something new or pursue a passion? NDIS can fund training programs, coaching, or even support you in finding a job or volunteering, helping you build skills and confidence.
  • Community Participation: The NDIS encourages getting out into the community. It can fund support for you to join social groups, sports, or other community activities so you can make friends and have fun.

Overall, the NDIS is there to help you live the life you choose – whether that’s mastering daily living skills, connecting with others, or simply feeling more confident and supported each day.

What Services and Supports Does the NDIS Cover?

NDIS covers a wide range of services and supports. Each participant’s plan is different, because supports are tailored to individual needs and goals. Here are some examples of what could be covered:

  • Personal Care: Help with daily activities like showering, dressing, eating, or moving around the house.
  • Therapies and Healthcare: Sessions with physiotherapists, speech therapists, occupational therapists, psychologists, or other specialists that help improve your health and abilities.
  • Assistive Technology: Equipment and devices such as wheelchairs, hearing aids, communication devices, or mobility scooters that help you with daily living.
  • Home and Vehicle Modifications: Changes to your home (like ramps, accessible bathrooms) or car (like hand controls or lifts) to make life easier and safer.
  • Community and Social Participation: Support workers to help you attend social events, join clubs, classes, or go on outings so you stay connected with your community.
  • Education and Employment Support: Assistance that helps you study or work, such as a support person at school or university, or on-the-job help to learn work skills.
  • Respite Care (Short-Term Accommodation): Funding for short stays away from home, or a support worker to come to your home, giving you a change of scenery and your family/carer a rest.

All these supports must be related to your disability and help you in daily life or to achieve your goals. The NDIS won’t cover things that everyone has to pay for (like general living expenses or medical costs not related to your disability), but it will cover those extra support needs that arise because of your disability. The key is that they are reasonable and necessary for you.

How Does the NDIS Support Carers?

Carers – whether they are parents, siblings, partners, or friends – play a huge role in supporting people with disabilities. The NDIS recognizes how important carers are and provides support to lighten their load, too. While the NDIS funds supports for the participant (the person with disability), these supports often give carers a much-needed break and peace of mind.

For example, the NDIS can fund respite care services. This means a trained support worker can step in for a few hours or days to care for your loved one, so that the primary carer can rest, attend appointments, or just recharge. Knowing that someone reliable is there to help can be a huge relief.

The NDIS can also include supports like training or counseling for carers, or provide equipment that makes caring at home easier (like hoists or shower chairs). Importantly, by helping the person with disability become more independent, the NDIS indirectly eases pressure on carers. When participants learn new skills or have support workers assisting them, carers can focus on their relationship (being a parent, spouse, friend) rather than only being a caregiver.

The bottom line is: the NDIS aims to support the whole family. As a carer, you should feel empowered to take breaks and look after your own wellbeing, knowing that support is available for your loved one.

How to Start Your NDIS Journey

Beginning your NDIS journey might seem a bit daunting, but don’t worry – you’re not alone, and there are plenty of resources to help. Here’s a simple step-by-step guide to get you started:

  1. Check Your Eligibility: The NDIS is generally for Australian citizens or residents under 65 who have a permanent and significant disability. If you think you meet these criteria, you’re off to a good start!
  2. Make an Access Request: Contact the National Disability Insurance Agency (NDIA) – you can call them or fill out a form on the NDIS website. This is basically your application to join the scheme. You may need to provide evidence of your disability (like medical or specialist reports).
  3. Meet Your Planner or LAC: If your application is accepted, you’ll meet with an NDIS planner or a Local Area Coordinator. This is a friendly conversation about your life, what support you have, and what you might need. It’s also a chance to talk about your goals – big or small – for now and the future.
  4. Create Your Plan: Together, you’ll develop an NDIS plan tailored to you. This plan will list the supports that the NDIS will fund for you. Don’t worry, you’ll get a copy of the plan and everything will be explained. Ask as many questions as you need – the plan is all about you.
  5. Start Using Your Supports: Once you have your plan, you can choose service providers (like HD Heed or others) to deliver the supports. For example, if your plan includes personal care assistance, you can arrange for a support worker from a provider to come help you at home. If you have funding for therapy, you can find therapists who are NDIS-registered to work with you.
  6. Review and Adjust: NDIS plans usually last about 12 months. Near the end of your plan, there will be a review meeting to see what’s working and if anything needs to change. You can adjust your supports in the next plan if your needs or goals evolve. And remember, you can always request a plan review earlier if something big in your life changes.

Starting out can be as simple as making a phone call to the NDIA or visiting the NDIS website to get the ball rolling. It might feel like a lot of information at first, but you can take it one step at a time. Many people also talk to local community organizations or providers like HD Heed for guidance through the process. We’re all here to help you navigate and succeed in your NDIS journey.

In conclusion, the NDIS is more than just a funding scheme – it’s a pathway to a more supported and independent life for people with disabilities, and a source of relief and empowerment for their carers. It’s normal to have lots of questions when you’re new to the NDIS, but take it step by step. You’ll soon discover the positive changes and opportunities that can come your way.

Remember, every NDIS journey is unique, just as every person is unique. Embrace the supports available, dream big about your goals, and don’t be afraid to ask for help when you need it. With the NDIS and caring people ready to back you up, you’re on the road to a brighter, more inclusive future for you and your family.
