In-Home Carer Relief

Respite that supports both carers and participants.

What Is In-Home Carer Relief?

Also known as respite care, in-home carer relief gives your primary carers a short break while you continue receiving essential support at home. This helps reduce stress for carers and supports your wellbeing in a familiar environment. Whether it’s for a few hours, overnight, or a weekend, a trained support worker steps in to help with your daily needs.

How It Works

Before starting respite care, we work with you and your carer to understand your routines and care requirements. A plan is developed for the scheduled time. Our support worker comes to your home and assists with personal care, mobility, medication, meals, supervision, and companionship — all aligned with your usual routine.

What Support Is Provided?

  • Personal care (e.g., showering, grooming, toileting)
  • Meal preparation and light housekeeping
  • Medication assistance and reminders
  • Mobility and transfer support
  • Social interaction and activities
  • Optional short outings if pre-arranged

Why Respite Care Matters

This service benefits both the participant and their carer. For carers, it prevents burnout and gives them time to rest, attend appointments, or enjoy personal time. For participants, it’s a chance to engage with someone new and maintain consistent care in the comfort of home.

Flexible Options

We offer short-term, regular, or emergency respite. Duration can range from a couple of hours to overnight or multi-day care. We ensure continuity by assigning a consistent team who follow your care plan closely, minimising disruption.

A Balanced Approach

NDIS-funded respite allows families to maintain sustainable caregiving over the long term. At HD Heed, we provide this support with compassion, reliability, and professionalism — giving your carer peace of mind while you remain well cared for at home.
