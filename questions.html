Universal Standards (use on every call)

  • Tone Warm, unhurried, person-centred. Avoid jargon unless the caller uses it. Mirror their language gently.
  • Speed 140–160 wpm. Pause to check understanding.
  • Structure Greet → Purpose → Permission → Discovery → Offer → Next steps → Confirm.
  • Compliance Privacy, consent, and accurate notes every time (NDIS-aligned). If in doubt, capture & escalate.
  • Empathy Validate feelings; acknowledge effort; focus on goals and outcomes.
Golden line: “Thanks for calling HD HeeD — how can we make things easier for you today?”
“I’m here with you.” • “Take your time.” • “I’ll summarise back to make sure I’ve got it right.”

General Intake Script (default)

Use for most inbound calls 
👋 “Good <time-of-day>, you’ve reached HD HeeD, this is <name>. How can I help today?”
🔐 “Before we begin, is it okay if I take a few details so we can support you properly?”
🧭 “What would you like help with right now?” (Listen. Reflect back a short summary.)
✅ “We can help with <service>. We’re NDIS registered and clinically led. The next step is to understand your goals and supports, then line up the right team.”
🧾 “Can I grab your full name, best contact, suburb, and NDIS number (if handy)?”
🧩 Discovery (see Data Capture). Check safety, timeframes, preferences.
📌 “Here’s what I can do for you today: <action>. We can start <when>.”
🗓 “Would you like me to email or text a summary with next steps?”
🤝 “Thanks for calling — you’re in good hands. Anything else I can answer?”

Call Structure — checkpoints

  1. Opening & permission — who, why, consent to take notes.
  2. Discovery — goals, current supports, risks, preferences, timeframes.
  3. Match — what we can do; realistic start dates; expectations.
  4. Commit — referrals, documentation, consent, next steps.
  5. Close — confirm details, recap, how to reach us.
Immediate risk • Clinical complexity • Safeguarding concerns
Urgent same-day • Standard 1–2 business days • Non-urgent 3–5 days

Data Capture Checklist

  • Participant legal name; preferred name; DOB; pronouns (optional).
  • Best phone & email; OK to SMS?
  • Suburb & postcode; living situation; access constraints at home.
  • NDIS number; plan dates; plan manager (or self/agency managed); budget category(s) & capacity.
  • Primary goals; priorities; days/times preferred; cultural or language needs; interpreter required.
  • Existing supports; current provider(s); reason for change if transitioning.
  • Risks/alerts (falls, behaviours, pressure injuries, seizures, allergies); equipment/AT.
  • Referrer details (SC, family, hospital); consent to share updates.

Tip: confirm spelling aloud for names/suburbs; repeat NDIS number in pairs.

Consent & Privacy Lines

Use matter-of-fact, friendly phrasing. Obtain consent before collecting or sharing personal info.

Collecting details (participant on the line)
Line: “I’ll note your details to arrange supports. We keep your information private and use it only for care. Is that okay to proceed?”
Third party (family/SC) — verbal consent
Line: “Do we have <participant’s name> on the line to confirm they’re happy for you to speak on their behalf? If not, I can take general info now and organise a quick consent call or consent form.”
Sharing updates with referrers
Line: “Would you like us to share progress updates with <name>? We’ll only share what’s needed to coordinate your supports.”
Recording consent in notes
• Verbal consent: Y/N, who gave it, date/time
• Scope: intake only / ongoing updates / full coordination
• Review/expiry date for consent

Triage & Safeguarding

  • Check for immediate risks (harm, medical crisis, lack of essential care).
  • If urgent: stay on the line, call 000 or relevant crisis line; escalate internally (clinical lead).
  • Record exact statements, time, and actions taken.
Urgent cue: “Are you or anyone with you in immediate danger, or without essential care today?”
If yes → follow emergency pathway, then document and notify clinical lead.

FAQs — Perfect Answers (Full Set)

Are you NDIS registered and what services do you offer?
Yes — HD HeeD is an NDIS registered provider. We support daily personal activities, domestic assistance, community participation, skill building, in-home respite, and clinically led nursing care at home. Share your goals and suburb; I’ll confirm what we can start and when.
How soon can services start?
Standard supports often begin within days once we confirm details and the Service Agreement. Complex/nursing supports may require a brief clinician review first. I’ll give you the earliest realistic start window now and watch for any earlier openings.
How do payments work for NDIA-managed, plan-managed, and self-managed?
• NDIA-managed: we create a service booking and claim directly — no out-of-pocket.
• Plan-managed: we invoice your Plan Manager.
• Self-managed: we invoice you; you then claim from the NDIS portal. Our rates follow the NDIS Pricing Arrangements.
What about travel, public holidays, or short-notice cancellations?
We follow NDIS rules. Any allowable travel or public holiday loadings are listed upfront in your Service Agreement. We aim to reschedule where possible to avoid cancellation fees.
Will I get the same worker each time?
We prioritise continuity and build a small, consistent team. We’ll also set a backup so there’s coverage for leave or illness, with advance notice whenever possible.
Can I choose worker gender, language, or cultural background?
We’ll do our best to match your preferences while keeping safety and quality front-of-mind. Tell me what matters to you and I’ll note it for rostering.
Do you talk to my Support Coordinator or Plan Manager?
Yes — with your consent we coordinate with your Support Coordinator and Plan Manager so invoicing and service bookings are smooth and aligned to your goals.
I don’t know my NDIS number — can we still proceed?
Absolutely. We can start with your contact details and suburb. Your NDIS number helps link your plan later — we can confirm it with you or your SC/PM.
Can you help with reports for a plan review?
Yes. We can summarise supports delivered, progress toward goals, and any unmet needs, and provide notes for your review meeting — with your consent.
What happens in an emergency or after hours?
If there’s danger or a medical emergency, call 000. For urgent non-emergencies, we follow our escalation pathway and place priority notes for the on-call/next business day team. We’ll agree on your preferred contact method during intake.

Support Coordinator Referral

Caller is SC / professional 
👋 “Hi, thanks for calling HD HeeD — <name> here. Are you referring a participant today?”
🔐 “With the participant’s consent, I’ll note details to progress intake quickly.”
🧾 “Participant full name, DOB, NDIS number, management type, plan dates, and current budget capacity?”
🎯 “Top goals/priorities right now? Any risks/behaviours/alerts?”
📍 “Suburb and preferred schedule? Worker preferences (gender/language/culture)?”
📨 “Please email referral notes and risk info to info@hdheed.com.au. With the participant’s consent, we’ll keep you updated.”
📌 “Earliest start for <service> in <suburb> is <window>. If clinician review is needed, I’ll prioritise that and confirm today.”
Perfect answer — “Can you start this week?”
“I’ll check local capacity now and hold the first suitable slot. If a clinical sign-off is required, I’ll fast-track it and confirm a realistic start time today.”
Quick quote & line items (what to say)
“We follow NDIS Pricing Arrangements. I can send a quote aligned to plan dates and weekly hours, including any allowable travel. Once accepted, we’ll lock in the schedule and service booking where applicable.”

🧾 “Can I take a few details to get things moving? Name, best contact, suburb, and NDIS number if you have it.”

🛟 “Any immediate risks or care gaps today?”

Perfect answer — “What will it cost me?”

Building confidence for first visits
“We can arrange a meet-and-greet, keep the same worker for continuity, and agree on a simple plan for the first two weeks with an early check-in.”

Complex Needs / Behaviours of Concern

Escalate to clinical if needed 
👋 “Thanks for sharing — I’ll take this carefully.”
🧠 “Could you outline any behaviours of concern, known triggers, positive strategies that work, and any safety plans?”
🔐 “Are any restrictive practices in place or proposed? Do you have a current Behaviour Support Plan we should review?”
🏥 “Health conditions, medications, allergies, seizure/falls history?”
🏠 “Home environment and equipment/AT? Who else is involved day-to-day?”
📌 “We’ll involve our clinical lead, match workers with the right experience, and confirm a realistic start date after that step.”
Perfect answer — “Have you handled a case like this?”
“Yes — we outline a similar pathway (no identifiers), the team composition, and training. If anything is new, we obtain training and clinical guidance before starting.”
Staff competency & emergency response (what to say)
“We complete an RN assessment, a written care plan, worker training with buddy shifts, and set clear escalation steps including when to call 000. Incidents are reviewed and improvements are fed back quickly.”
24/7 or 2-person support
“We build a small, consistent roster with trained relief and use fatigue-aware scheduling. You’ll know everyone on the team.”
Consent & safeguarding lines
“We’ll only share information with people you choose. If there’s a safety concern, we act quickly and document exactly what happened and what we did.”

Hospital Discharge

Time-sensitive coordination 
🏥 “Let’s line everything up so home feels safe on day one.”
📅 “What’s the expected discharge date/time and ward contact?”
📄 “Can we receive the discharge summary, allied health recommendations, and any risk alerts?”
🏠 “Home setup: access, bed/bath layout, equipment/AT status, and the support hours needed in the first 72 hours.”
🧑‍⚕️ “If a clinician review is needed, I’ll prioritise it and hold immediate slots around discharge.”

Perfect answers (common objections)

“Can you start within 48 hours?”
“We’ll prioritise your case. If helpful, we can connect with the ward pre-discharge for a direct handover and schedule day-one supports. I’ll confirm exact times today.”
“AT/home modifications aren’t ready.”
“We’ll implement safe interim strategies (extra staffing/alternate techniques) and coordinate with OT/supplier on delivery dates.”
“We’ll ramp down after week one.”
“Great — we’ll document an initial higher-support period and a taper plan as recovery progresses.”

Day 0–7 checklist

  • RN assessment within 24–48h; vitals & wound check if applicable.
  • Medication chart set-up; explain red-flag symptoms & who to call.
  • Home safety walk-through; emergency numbers visible.
  • Follow-up visits booked; GP review coordinated if advised.

Nursing-Only Request

Clinically led 
🩺 “You’re after nursing support — noted. What’s the primary clinical task or goal?”
🧾 “Diagnosis/conditions; current meds; GP/specialist; any recent changes or discharge?”
🩹 “Any wound care, injections, PEG/catheter care, monitoring, or carer training needed?”
📅 “Frequency and preferred days/times; any access notes at the home?”
📌 “I’ll book an RN assessment if appropriate and confirm the earliest visit today.”

Nursing FAQs

RN vs EN and competencies
“We deploy RNs for community visits; ENs may support under RN supervision. We ensure task-specific competencies and liaise with your GP/specialists.”
Billing & line items
“We use the appropriate NDIS community nursing line items. Rates follow the Pricing Arrangements; I can send a clear quote and plan-aligned schedule.”
Training caregivers
“Where appropriate, our RN can train family/support workers with written instructions and supervision boundaries.”

Short dialogue

Caller: Need wound dressings 3× weekly for 2 weeks.
Staff: I’ll book an RN for tomorrow, confirm supplies, and align with your GP plan. We’ll taper as healing progresses.
