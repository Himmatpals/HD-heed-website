Daily Living Skills Support

Empowering you with the skills to live independently.

Learn Skills for a More Independent Life

Daily living skills support (sometimes called life skills training or capacity building) is a service that helps you learn and improve the skills you need for everyday independence. Instead of doing things for you, our support workers or mentors will work with you to practice and build confidence in a variety of tasks and routines. The ultimate aim is to empower you to do more on your own, making day-to-day life easier and more fulfilling.

What kinds of skills are we talking about? It can cover an array of areas – essentially anything that will help you live more autonomously. Common focuses include:

  • Personal care skills: Learn how to safely shower, dress yourself with minimal help, and manage your grooming and hygiene independently.
  • Home care skills: Tasks like cleaning, laundry, washing dishes, basic cooking, and other household chores in a way that works for you.
  • Meal preparation: Planning meals, grocery shopping on a budget, cooking simple recipes, or attending a cooking class with support.
  • Money management: Handling personal finances, budgeting NDIS funds or pensions, paying bills, and using ATMs or banking apps.
  • Social and communication skills: Building confidence in social interactions, handling phone calls/emails, understanding social cues, and forming friendships.
  • Travel skills: Using public transport, reading timetables, safely crossing roads, or practicing how to use rideshare or taxis.
  • Time management and routine planning: Learning to follow daily schedules, using calendars or reminder apps, and structuring your day effectively.

This support is very personalized – you might focus on a few key skills that align with your goals. For instance, if one of your goals is to live independently, we would prioritize cooking, cleaning, and budgeting. If your goal is to be more social, we might work on conversation skills, joining community activities, and gaining confidence in public settings.

How is the training delivered?

Life skills support can happen one-on-one at your home or in the community, or in a small group/workshop setting, depending on your comfort level. A support worker typically teaches through guided practice – doing the activity with you and gradually stepping back as your confidence builds. For example, they might spend an afternoon baking with you, guiding you through the recipe so you gradually learn to do it independently.

Sessions are hands-on, fun, and engaging – not formal. We use visual prompts, real-life scenarios, and break tasks into manageable steps. Over time, you’ll likely find that you need less help and can perform tasks on your own. We also incorporate problem-solving scenarios to prepare you for unexpected situations.

Benefits of Life Skills Development

  • Improved independence and confidence in daily routines.
  • Increased self-esteem as you gain the ability to care for yourself and manage your life.
  • Reduced reliance on others for basic tasks.
  • Improved safety through learning the right way to do things.
  • Preparation for bigger goals like work or independent living.

Whether it’s learning how to manage your money, cook meals, or navigate public transport, life skills support helps you gain the confidence and ability to thrive on your own terms. And families can enjoy peace of mind knowing their loved ones are becoming more independent.
